Existing English summary (use as anchor, expand significantly):
---
People often ask freelancers: "Isn't it lonely?"
My honest answer: yes. But I no longer think loneliness is the enemy.

The first months of working alone are louder in their silence than
anyone warned me. No morning greeting. No lunch invitation. No
"good work today" at the end of the day. Several people I know
returned to corporate jobs because of exactly this.

What helped me settle into it:

1. Step outside in the morning — a walk, a convenience store, five
   minutes. Feeling other humans' presence makes home's quiet feel
   chosen, not imposed.
2. See someone once a week. Not for work. Coffee is enough.
3. Use AI as a thinking partner. Modern luxury — not a person, but
   it prevents the spiral of solo overthinking.

But more than those tactics, my view of loneliness has shifted.
Without quiet hours, you can't think deeply. Without time alone,
your own voice fades. In corporate life I spent most of my
"thinking time" in conversation with others. Working alone built
the muscle of thinking just with myself.

You don't need to eliminate loneliness. You only need to let it
sit alongside the other thing — the ability to hear yourself
clearly. Solo work is both, at once.
