If asked to name one Toyama winter dish, I'd say "buri daikon" without
hesitation — winter yellowtail simmered slowly with thick-cut daikon
radish in soy, sugar, sake, and ginger.

Nothing exotic in the technique. But on a cold evening, opening the
front door to that smell changes the temperature of the whole house.

Toyama Bay is one of Japan's prime winter yellowtail grounds.
"Himi kanburi" — Himi-port winter yellowtail — is a brand-graded fish.
The trimmings sold cheap at local supermarkets carry even more fat
than the prime cuts. Simmered with daikon, they make a home dish
that rivals any restaurant version.

The exact seasoning ratio varies family to family. My household goes
heavier on sugar, generous on ginger. Other houses go salt-forward.

how Mom's kitchen ran when you were small.

Living in Toyama means making this dish every winter. Not a tourist
food. Just the kitchen of where I live.

■ 我が家のぶり大根

私の家のぶり大根は、シンプルだ。

大根は太い輪切り。下茹でしてアク抜き。
ブリのアラは、湯通しして血合いを洗う。
鍋に大根、ブリ、しょうゆ、砂糖、酒、生姜の薄切り、水を入れて、弱火で40分。

味付けの黄金比は、家ごとに微妙に違う。
うちは砂糖をしっかりめ、生姜は多めだ。
