In central Takaoka sits "Takaoka Castle Park" — what looks like a
city walking park is actually the grounds of a brief 17th-century
castle.

Maeda Toshinaga, the Kaga clan's second lord, built the castle in
1609. Six years later, the Tokugawa shogunate's "one castle per

moats and stone walls stayed.

The clan repurposed the site as rice storehouses and warehouses

space, in keeping with Kaga's thrift culture.

Today, four centuries later, it's an ordinary urban park: walkers
and joggers in morning, cherry blossoms in spring, deep shade in

Toshinaga, the city museum, a small zoo, and Imizu Shrine.
