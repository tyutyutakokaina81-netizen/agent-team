## Toyama 2–3 Day Itinerary — A Printable Planner

*A local's plan for the Sea-of-Japan side of the Japan Alps: Toyama City, Takaoka, Himi & the coast. Honest, flexible, no hype.*

**How to use this planner**
- Print it, fold it, keep it in your pocket. Everything fits on a few sheets.
- No addresses are listed. Under each stop you'll find **Google Maps search words** — type them in and the pin will find you.
- Times are approximate and change by season and service. Confirm trains and opening periods with official sources before you go.
- Base yourself in **Toyama City** for the whole trip. Its train links let you do all three days without changing hotels (move one night to an onsen or seaside inn only if you want variety).

---

### Arrival (the afternoon/evening you get in)

| Time | Plan |
|---|---|
| On arrival | Check in around Toyama City. Pick up a transit IC card if you don't have one. |
| Evening | An easy first dinner: white shrimp (shiroebi), sushi, or kelp-cured fish. Walk it off along the canal park. |

- **Getting here**: ~2 hrs from Tokyo by Hokuriku Shinkansen; ~20 min from Kanazawa.
- **Rainy arrival?** The canal-side café and the glass art museum both work in any weather (see Day 3).
- **Google Maps search words**: `Toyama Station` · `Toyama Glass Art Museum` · `Fugan Unga Kansui Park`

### Day 1 — The coast, craft & the manga trail

*Takaoka and Himi: 400-year-old metal craft, a giant bronze Buddha, a working fishing port, and — on a clear day — the Alps rising out of the sea.*

| Time | Plan | One line |
|---|---|---|
| Morning | Train to **Takaoka**. Walk the Kanayamachi metal-casters' street; see the Great Buddha. | Quiet, photogenic, 400 years of bronze. |
| Late morning | Optional: the calm Zen temple **Zuiryuji**, and gentle **Doraemon** touches around town (the creator's hometown). | Serene, and a smile for manga fans. |
| Lunch | Local **JR Himi Line** to **Himi**. Seafood lunch at the port. | Often the best meal of the trip. |
| Afternoon | Walk the **Himi coastline**. On a clear day the Tateyama mountains float over the bay. Train back. | Mountains-over-sea is the money shot. |

- **Getting around**: Kanazawa→Takaoka ~15–40 min depending on the train; Takaoka→Himi ~30 min on the local JR Himi Line.
- **Food candidates**: white shrimp (shiroebi) — sweet "jewel of the bay"; in winter, cold yellowtail (kanburi) as sashimi or hot-pot; pressed-kelp sashimi (kombu-jime), a Toyama specialty.
- **Rainy-day swap**: skip the open coastline; spend longer in Takaoka's covered craft workshops, the Great Buddha, and Zuiryuji, then a long seafood lunch in Himi.
- **Google Maps search words**: `Kanayamachi Takaoka` · `Great Buddha of Takaoka` · `Zuiryuji Temple` · `Himi Station` · `Himi fishing port` · `Amaharashi Coast`

---

### Day 2 — The mountains (pick by season)

*Toyama's big-scenery day. Choose the option that fits the season; both are memorable.*

**Option A (spring–autumn): the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route**

| Time | Plan |
|---|---|
| Early AM | Local train from Toyama to the foot of the Alpine Route. Reserve tickets ahead. |
| Late AM | Cable car + ropeway up to Murodo (~2,450 m). In spring, walk the "snow corridor." |
| Lunch | A simple meal at altitude, with alpine views. |
| Afternoon | Easy high-altitude walks (ponds, viewpoints), then descend the same way to Toyama. |
| Evening | Dinner back in the city — or move to an onsen inn for the night. |

**Option B (winter, or a gentler day): gorge & thatched villages**

- Ride the scenic Kurobe Gorge trolley train (warmer months), or visit the UNESCO thatched villages of **Gokayama** — quiet, storybook, stunning under snow. Either pairs well with an onsen evening.

- **Note**: the high Alpine Route is seasonal and closes in winter. Check opening dates before you plan Day 2 around it.
- **Rainy-day swap**: high mountains are poor in heavy rain/cloud — switch to Gokayama villages or a gorge/onsen day.
- **Food candidates**: a simple mountain lunch up top; a proper local dinner (Toyama Black ramen, sake) back in the city.
- **Google Maps search words**: `Tateyama Station Toyama` · `Murodo Tateyama` · `Kurobe Gorge Railway` · `Gokayama Ainokura` · `Gokayama Suganuma`

---

### Day 3 — City, glass, onsen & food

*After a big mountain day, slow the pace right down.*

| Time | Plan | One line |
|---|---|---|
| Morning | **Toyama Glass Art Museum** (a striking Kengo Kuma building) + a tram stroll through the city. | Design lovers linger here. |
| Midday | Seafood lunch — white shrimp, sushi, kelp-cured fish. | The bay, one more time. |
| Afternoon | **Fugan Unga Kansui Park** and its canal-side café; or, if you missed it, the Doraemon & Hattori-kun manga trail. | Prettiest café view in the city. |
| Late afternoon | A final soak — a city onsen, or check into a hot-spring inn if you're staying on. | The restful bookend. |
| Evening | Relaxed last dinner; grab kelp and shrimp crackers as souvenirs. | Edible souvenirs travel well. |

- **Getting around**: Toyama City's trams and short walks cover everything on Day 3.
- **Rainy-day swap**: this is already the all-weather day — the museum, café and onsen are all indoors.
- **Food candidates**: white shrimp (shiroebi), sushi, kombu-jime; Toyama Black ramen for something hearty; local sake.
- **Google Maps search words**: `Toyama Glass Art Museum` · `Fugan Unga Kansui Park` · `Toyama tram` · `Toyama Castle Park`

---

### If you have a 4th night

Branch out: a night near **Kanazawa** (20 min away) for gardens and old districts, or push toward Takayama and the Alps.

### Short on time? A 1-day version

Only have a day, perhaps from Kanazawa? Do the Day 1 coast-and-culture loop (Takaoka + Himi) on its own — it stands alone as a full, satisfying day.
