# Fugan Unga Kansui Park and Its Canal-Side Cafe

I live in Takaoka, one city over, and Fugan Unga Kansui Park in Toyama is one of the places I keep coming back to on quiet mornings. If you searched for "Toyama Kansui Park Starbucks canal walk," you have probably already seen the photos: a wide canal, a white bridge, and a coffee shop with glass on three sides. This is a walk-through from someone who has done the loop many times, in different seasons and at different hours, so you can decide whether it fits your trip.

## What the park actually is

Kansui Park is built around a stretch of the old Fugan Canal, close to Toyama Station on the north side. You can walk there in roughly ten to fifteen minutes from the station, though the exact time depends on your pace and which exit you use — worth checking on a map before you go. It is a flat, open park, so it is easy for families, older visitors, and anyone who does not want a hilly walk.

The canal is the centerpiece. There is a broad water basin, lawns, and paved paths on both banks, so you can make a loop rather than doubling back. It is a city park, not wilderness, so expect joggers, dog walkers, and on weekends a fair number of people with cameras.

## The canal-side cafe

The Starbucks here gets written about a lot, and I understand why. It sits low next to the water with large windows, so from inside you look straight out over the canal and the bridge. On a calm day the water reflects the sky, and that is the view most people come for. It is a regular branch with the usual menu, so the draw is the setting rather than anything unusual on the drinks list.

A practical note: because it is popular, seats by the window fill up, especially on weekend afternoons and in cherry-blossom season. If a window seat matters to you, earlier in the day is a safer bet. You can also take a drink outside and sit on the steps or a bench by the water, which I often prefer.

## Tenmon Bridge and the view

Tenmon Bridge is the white pedestrian bridge that arches over the canal. You can walk across it, and from the middle you get the long view down the water — the cafe on one side, the basin opening out on the other. There are said to be small observation spaces at the ends of the bridge that you can go up into; I have gone up on a clear day and the view over the park is pleasant, though whether they are open can depend on the day and season, so treat that as something to confirm on site.

On a clear day some people say you can see the mountains beyond the city from up there. I have had days where the ridgeline was sharp and days where it was hazy, so I would not promise it. Winter and early morning tend to give the cleaner air.

## The water line boats

There is a small boat service, the Fugan Suijo Line, that runs on the canal and connects this park with another park further along the water. When it is running it is a relaxed way to see the canal from the water, and there is a lock section on the route that some passengers find the most interesting part. Schedules and whether it operates change with the season and weather, and it can require booking on busy days, so check the current timetable before you count on it — I would call that a plan-ahead item rather than a walk-up one.

## Morning or evening?

This is the question I get asked most. Morning is quieter, the light is soft, the cafe is easier to get a seat in, and the air is usually clearer for the mountain view. Evening has its own appeal: on many nights the bridge and park are lit up, and the reflections on the water look good after dark. If you only have one slot and you want calm and clear views, I lean morning. If you want the illumination and do not mind more people, go in the evening. Both are worth it; they are just different moods.
