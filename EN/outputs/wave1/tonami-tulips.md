# Tonami Tulip Fair: When and How to Go

I live in Takaoka, one city over from Tonami, and every spring a few friends visiting from abroad ask me the same thing: when is the tulip fair, and can they reach it without a car? Here is what I actually tell them.

## When it happens

The Tonami Tulip Fair (となみチューリップフェア) runs for a short stretch that usually starts in late April and ends in early May, overlapping Japan's Golden Week holiday. The exact dates shift each year with the season and the calendar, so treat any figure you see as provisional until the organizers post it. For 2026, check the official fair site or the Tonami city tourism page before you lock in travel — I would not book non-refundable dates around a guess.

One honest caveat: tulips are living things. A warm March can push the peak bloom earlier than the posted fair dates, and a cold snap can hold it back. The fair opens on schedule regardless, but "fair is open" and "flowers are at their fullest" are not always the same week. If you can be flexible, aim for the middle of the run rather than day one.

## Getting there from Toyama by train

This is the part most overseas visitors get wrong, so I'll be specific. From Toyama Station, take the JR Johana Line (城端線) toward Johana and get off at Tonami Station. The ride is roughly 30 to 40 minutes depending on the service. The Johana Line is a local, single-track line — trains do not run every few minutes like a city subway. Gaps of 30 to 60 minutes between departures are normal, so photograph the timetable or save it offline before you go, and note the last train back.

From Tonami Station, the main fair site is a walk of very roughly 15 minutes on foot. During the fair there is usually additional signage and, in some years, shuttle support on the busiest days — that support varies year to year, so confirm it rather than counting on it.

If you are coming from farther out, the Shinkansen stops at Shin-Takaoka Station, which also sits on the Johana Line, so you can transfer there instead of routing through Toyama. Either works.

## Crowds and parking, honestly

Because the fair lands on Golden Week, the peak days — especially the weekend and the public holidays in early May — get genuinely busy. If you drive, the car parks near the site fill up, and the roads into Tonami back up in the late morning. I don't recommend arriving by car between about 10am and 1pm on a holiday unless you enjoy queuing.

This is exactly why I push visitors toward the train. You skip the parking problem entirely, and a mid-morning weekday visit is far calmer than a holiday afternoon. If your schedule only allows a peak day, go early — being on-site near opening buys you an hour or two before the heaviest crowds arrive.

## What to expect on site

It is a large planted field-and-park layout with a lot of walking, so wear real shoes. There is an admission fee (the amount changes year to year — check the current figure), and food stalls and indoor displays are usually part of the event. Weather in early May can swing from warm sun to cool wind, so a light layer is sensible.

## Fact-check note

- ✅ The fair is held in Tonami City, Toyama Prefecture, and typically runs across the late-April-to-early-May Golden Week window.
- ✅ Tonami is reachable from Toyama Station via the JR Johana Line; Shin-Takaoka is also on that line.
- ⚠️ Exact 2026 dates, admission price, and any shuttle service — confirm on the official fair/city site; these change annually.
- ⚠️ Peak-bloom timing versus the posted fair dates can differ with the weather; 諸説あります (accounts vary), so verify near your travel date.
- ⚠️ Johana Line frequency is limited — check the current timetable rather than assuming frequent service.

## Where to stay

Toyama City and Takaoka both make practical bases with train links to Tonami; for overseas visitors, general booking sites like Booking.com or Agoda are usually the easiest way to compare and reserve.
