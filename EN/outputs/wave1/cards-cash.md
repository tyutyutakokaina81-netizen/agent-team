# Cards or Cash in Toyama? ATMs That Take Foreign Cards

I live in Takaoka, in Toyama Prefecture on the coast of the Sea of Japan. Friends who visit from abroad almost always ask me the same thing before they arrive: can I just tap my card everywhere, or do I need to carry cash? The honest answer is somewhere in between. Toyama is more cash-friendly than a big city like Tokyo or Osaka, and a little planning saves you the awkward moment of standing at a counter that only takes yen notes.

Here is how it actually works, based on the places I use week to week.

## Where cards usually work fine

Chain places are your safe bet. Supermarkets, drugstores, larger convenience stores, department stores, and most hotels take Visa and Mastercard without any fuss. Many also take American Express and JCB, though not all. Big electronics stores and shopping-mall shops are the same. If you are booking a rental car or paying for a hotel, a card almost always works.

Contactless is spreading but uneven. Some registers accept tap-to-pay from your phone or card; others still ask you to insert and sign or enter a PIN. I would not assume contactless will work every time, so keep the physical card handy.

## Where it is often cash only

The places that surprise visitors are usually the small, good ones. Family-run soba and sushi shops, older ramen counters, tiny izakaya, some morning markets, and small craft or souvenir stalls frequently take cash only. Local buses and some smaller train lines also lean toward cash or a transit IC card rather than a credit card. A few small guesthouses and temples ask for cash too.

My rule of thumb: the more charming and independent the place looks, the more likely it is cash only. That is not a hard rule, just a pattern I have seen for years. When in doubt, ask "kaado, daijoubu desu ka?" (is a card okay?) before you order.

## ATMs that take foreign cards

This is the part most guides get vague about, so let me be specific.

**Convenience store ATMs** are the reliable ones. Seven Bank ATMs, found in 7-Eleven stores, accept most overseas Visa, Mastercard, and other network cards, with English on-screen menus. Lawson and FamilyMart ATMs (E-net and others) also handle many foreign cards, though I have heard occasional reports of a card being rejected at one machine and working fine at another, so this is worth confirming for your specific bank.

**Japan Post Bank ATMs** (Yucho), found in post offices and some stations, also accept many foreign cards and offer English menus. Post office hours can be limited, especially on weekends, so the machines inside a post office may not be available late. Convenience store ATMs tend to run longer hours, sometimes 24 hours in the busier locations.

You will find these ATMs across Takaoka, Toyama city, and around Himi, mostly near stations, main streets, and shopping areas. Smaller towns and quieter coastal spots have fewer of them, so I would withdraw cash before heading out to a rural area or a fishing village for the day.

A practical habit: carry maybe 10,000 to 20,000 yen in cash when you are out exploring, and treat cards as the backup for larger or chain purchases. That is the reverse of how many people travel at home, but it fits Toyama well.

## A few small tips

Tell your bank you will be in Japan so they do not freeze the card. Choose "without conversion" (pay in yen) at the ATM or register if you are asked, since the machine's own exchange rate is usually worse than your bank's. And keep a small stash of coins for lockers, small shrines, and vending machines.

## Fact-check note

- ✅ Seven Bank and Japan Post Bank ATMs are widely documented as accepting foreign cards with English menus.
- ✅ Small independent restaurants and stalls in Toyama are commonly cash only, in my direct experience.
- ⚠️ Which specific card networks (Amex, JCB, UnionPay, etc.) a given shop or ATM accepts changes over time and by location — confirm at the machine or ask staff.
- ⚠️ ATM operating hours vary by branch and by convenience-store location; "24 hours" is not guaranteed everywhere, so check the machine's own notice.
- ⚠️ Reports of a foreign card working at one convenience-store ATM but not another do circulate; results may vary by issuer, so I would not rely on a single machine.
