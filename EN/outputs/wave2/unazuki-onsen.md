# Unazuki Onsen: The Hot Spring Gateway to Kurobe Gorge

Most people who end up in Unazuki Onsen are not there for the town itself. They come for the Kurobe Gorge Railway — the little open-sided sightseeing train that crawls up the valley — and the hot spring town happens to sit right at its starting point. That combination is the whole appeal, and it's worth understanding before you plan a trip. I live nearby in Toyama, and I've made the trip in different seasons, so here's the practical version.

## The train and the town share one address, roughly

Unazuki sits at the eastern edge of Toyama, up where the mountains close in. It's the terminus of the local railway that brings you in from the coast side, and it's also where the Kurobe Gorge trolley train departs. So the town is compact and built around that role: a cluster of hot spring hotels, a few shops, footbaths, and a view over the river. You can walk the main stretch in fifteen minutes.

Because the town is small, don't expect a big entertainment district. Evenings are quiet. That's a feature if you want a soak-and-sleep trip, and a drawback if you're hoping for nightlife.

## Day trip vs. overnight — how I'd actually choose

**Do a day trip if** your main goal is the gorge railway and you're basing yourself in the city or on the coast. You can ride the trolley, use a public footbath or a day-use bath at one of the hotels, and head back. Several hotels open their baths to non-staying visitors during set daytime hours (higashi-yu, 日帰り入浴), though which ones and at what times changes, so I'd confirm the day you go rather than trust an old blog.

**Stay overnight if** you want the ryokan rhythm — an early gorge run before day-trippers arrive, then a long evening bath and a kaiseki dinner. The gorge train also has limited daily capacity and can sell out in peak foliage season, so staying in town gives you a better shot at an early departure. Autumn (roughly late October into November) is the busiest and, to my eye, the best-looking; book well ahead.

One honest caveat: the trolley railway is seasonal. It generally runs from around spring through late autumn and closes for winter, and the exact opening and closing dates shift each year with snow conditions. If you're coming for the train specifically, check the current operating calendar before locking in dates — arriving in the off-season means a hot spring town with its main attraction shut.

## The water and the baths

Unazuki's hot spring water is piped down from a source further up the valley. Locally it's described as clear and fairly gentle on the skin — a simple alkaline-type water rather than a strong, cloudy sulfur bath. That's my read from bathing there and from what's posted around town; if you have a specific mineral requirement, treat the exact composition as something to verify at your hotel.

There's an open footbath in the town center where you can sit with the river view for free, which is a nice low-commitment way to sample the place if you're only passing through.

## Getting the timing right

The gorge train is slow by design — a one-way run up the valley takes over an hour, and you'll want time at the upper stops. Combined with a bath, that's most of a day. If you're day-tripping, start early. If you're staying, you have room to breathe.

## Fact-check note

- ✅ Unazuki Onsen sits at the departure point of the Kurobe Gorge sightseeing railway, in eastern Toyama. This is well established and I've traveled it myself.
- ✅ The gorge railway is seasonal and closed in winter; it does not run year-round.
- ⚠️ Exact seasonal opening/closing dates for the railway shift each year — verify the current calendar.
- ⚠️ Which hotels offer day-use bathing, and their hours, change over time — confirm on the day.
- ⚠️ The precise mineral composition of the spring water: I'm describing the general local reputation, not a lab reading. Check with your accommodation if it matters to you.

## Where to stay

Unazuki is a small ryokan town, so rooms are limited and fill up in foliage season. Overseas visitors usually find Booking.com or Agoda the easiest way to compare the hotels and see which ones include the day-use or private baths they want.
