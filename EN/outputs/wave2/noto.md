# A Day Trip to the Noto Peninsula from Toyama

I live in Takaoka, on the Toyama side of the bay, and the Noto Peninsula has always felt like the coast next door rather than a far-off destination. If you are based in Toyama City or nearby and want to see a stretch of Noto without an overnight stay, it is doable — but I want to be honest about the distances and about the fact that the peninsula was hit by a large earthquake on January 1, 2024, and recovery is still uneven.

## How far you can realistically go

Toyama sits at the eastern base of the bay. The nearest part of Noto is the coast reachable through Himi, a fishing town about 30 to 40 minutes by train or car from central Takaoka, which is itself around 20 minutes from Toyama City by train. From Himi you cross into Ishikawa Prefecture and the southern, gentler side of the peninsula opens up.

For a day trip, I would keep to that southern and central stretch. The dramatic northern tip — the areas most people picture when they think of Noto's rugged cliffs — is a long drive further on, and it was also the hardest hit by the quake. Trying to reach the far north and get back to Toyama in a day leaves you rushing, and some northern roads and sites have had ongoing closures or reduced access. I would not promise a specific northern site is open; that is something to check close to your travel date.

## Getting there

A rental car gives you the most freedom, because the coastal roads and smaller villages are not well served by frequent buses. The drive from Toyama into the southern Noto coast is roughly an hour and a half to two hours depending on where you aim for. There are limited-express trains and some tourist services that run part of the way, but connections thin out once you are on the peninsula itself, so plan the return leg before you set off. Winter can bring snow and slower roads, so a summer or autumn trip is easier if you have the choice.

## What a day looks like

A calm, honest day trip for me is: leave Toyama mid-morning, stop in Himi for an early seafood lunch — the town is known locally for its fish, and the fishing culture is genuine rather than staged — then cross into the southern Noto coast for the afternoon. You get quiet bays, small ports, and views back across the water toward the Tateyama mountains on a clear day. It is unhurried scenery rather than a checklist of big sights, and I think that is its appeal.

Please treat some areas with care. This is a place where people are still rebuilding homes and livelihoods. Not everything is a tourist backdrop, and a few businesses that were open before the quake may not have reopened.

## Fact-check note

- ✅ Himi is roughly 30–40 minutes from central Takaoka and sits on the Toyama-side approach into Noto.
- ✅ A magnitude-7 class earthquake struck the Noto Peninsula on January 1, 2024, with the northern tip most affected.
- ⚠️ Specific road, rail, and site openings on the peninsula change over time — verify current access close to your trip, as recovery is ongoing.
- ⚠️ Drive times I give are rough and depend on your exact start and destination; treat them as estimates, not schedules.
- ⚠️ Some seasonal tourist train services run only on certain days — confirm timetables before relying on them.

## Where to stay

Most trips from here work fine as a same-day return to Toyama. If you would rather stay a night on either side, overseas visitors usually find Booking.com or Agoda the easiest places to compare rooms.
