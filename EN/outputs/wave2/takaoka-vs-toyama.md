# Takaoka or Toyama City: Which to Base Yourself In

I live in Takaoka, and I regularly take the short train ride over to Toyama City. People planning a trip to this stretch of the Japan Sea coast keep asking me the same thing: which of the two makes a better base? The honest answer is that it depends on what you want your mornings and evenings to look like. Here is how I actually think about it.

## The number of hotels near the station

Toyama City is the prefectural capital, and its main station area simply has more places to sleep. Business hotels, a few mid-range options, and chains cluster within a short walk of the station. If you land in the region without a firm plan and want to just pick something the night before, Toyama gives you more choices in one compact area.

Takaoka has hotels near its station too, but fewer of them, and they book up faster during festival periods or busy weekends. If you have your heart set on staying in Takaoka, I would reserve earlier rather than assume you can walk in. That is not a knock on the town — it is just a smaller pool.

## Which station gets you moving faster

This is where Takaoka earns its place. Takaoka Station is the practical jumping-off point for two things a lot of overseas visitors come here for: the tram-and-train line that runs toward the coast and Himi, and the local line that heads out toward the Etchu area. If your plan leans toward the sea, small fishing towns, and slower coastal scenery, waking up in Takaoka shaves real time and transfers off your day.

Toyama City is the better hub if your trip is wider. It is the connecting point for the main Shinkansen, for the well-known mountain route that opens seasonally, and for lines heading in several directions. If you are treating this area as one stop on a longer Japan itinerary, or you want to fan out to different day trips, the capital keeps your options open.

## How I actually advise people

If your interests are the coast, Himi, casting-net views of the bay, and Takaoka's own historic core, base yourself in Takaoka and accept the smaller hotel selection — book ahead. If you want maximum flexibility, easy Shinkansen access, and a bigger menu of rooms and evening food within a few minutes of your bag, base yourself in Toyama City and treat Takaoka as an easy day trip. The two are close enough that neither choice locks you out of the other.

One more honest note: both stations are walkable and calm compared with Japan's big cities. Neither is a nightlife destination. If lively evenings matter to you, set expectations accordingly — this is a quieter coast, and for many travellers that is exactly the appeal.

## Fact-check note

- ✅ Toyama City is the prefectural capital and a Shinkansen stop; its station area has a larger concentration of accommodation than Takaoka's.
- ✅ Takaoka Station connects to the local lines running toward the coast and Himi.
- ⚠️ Exact hotel counts, current room availability, and seasonal route openings change — confirm before booking, especially around festivals and holidays.
- ⚠️ Which specific lines and services run on a given day can vary; check current timetables rather than relying on this page.
