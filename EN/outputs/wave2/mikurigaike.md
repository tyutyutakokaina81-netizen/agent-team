# Mikurigaike Pond: The Easy Alpine Walk with a Reflection View

I live in Takaoka, and Tateyama is close enough that I go up a few times each summer. People often ask me the same thing: is there a spot high in the mountains where you can see the water mirror the peaks, but without a hard climb? Mikurigaike Pond is the honest answer. It sits right by Murodo, the highest point most visitors reach by public transport, and on a calm morning it holds a clear reflection of the ridgeline. You do not need to be fit to reach it. You just need to know a few real things before you go.

## Getting there and what the ground is like

Murodo is the end of the line for the buses and cable car that carry you up from either the Toyama side or the Nagano side. When you step off, you are already at roughly 2,450 meters, so the pond is not a climb — it is a short walk down and around from the terminal building. Signposts in English point the way, and the pond comes into view within about ten to fifteen minutes of easy walking for most people.

The path itself is mostly a paved or stone-laid trail, wide and clearly marked. There are gentle ups and downs and some steps, so it is not a flat sidewalk, but it does not demand scrambling or long climbing. If you can manage a slow walk with a few staircases, you can reach a good viewpoint over the water.

## The reflection, and when you actually see it

The reflection is real, but it is not guaranteed. Mikurigaike is a crater lake, and its surface only turns into a proper mirror when the air is still. Early morning, before the day's wind picks up, tends to be your best chance — that is the pattern I have noticed on my own visits, though I cannot promise any single morning will be calm. If a breeze is up, you still get a beautiful deep-blue pond ringed by mountains, just without the sharp mirror image. Manage your expectations and treat a perfect reflection as a bonus, not a certainty.

## Taking it slow at altitude

The one thing people underestimate is the height. At around 2,400 meters the air is thinner, and even the gentle walk can leave you a little breathless if you rush. Take it slowly, drink water, and give yourself a minute to adjust after stepping off the bus. This is genuinely why the pond suits people who are not confident hikers: the distance is short, so you can go at a crawl and still get there.

Bring a layer even in summer. It is noticeably cooler up top than down in the city, and weather can shift. The terminal building has toilets, food, and shelter, which makes the whole outing manageable for a half-day rather than a full expedition.

## A loose walking loop

Many visitors just go to the pond and back, which is fine. There is also a broader network of well-trodden paths around the Murodo area that let you make a gentle loop past the pond and other viewpoints. How far you go is up to your energy that day — you can turn back at any point, since you are never far from the terminal.

## Fact-check note

- ✅ Murodo is the highest point reached by the region's regular bus/cable transport, and Mikurigaike is a short, mostly paved walk from it.
- ✅ Murodo sits at roughly 2,450 meters, so altitude and cooler temperatures are real factors even in summer.
- ⚠️ The best time for a still reflection: early morning is commonly said to be calmest, but wind is unpredictable — treat a mirror view as luck, not a plan. Confirm on the day.
- ⚠️ Exact walking times and trail conditions vary by season and by how much snow remains; check current transport and trail status before you set out.
