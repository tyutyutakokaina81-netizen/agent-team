## X1（一次観察・情景描写型）
> On a clear morning at Amaharashi, the water goes flat and the 3,000m Tateyama range looks like it's floating on top of the sea. Locals just call it "a good morning." No filter needed — you do need the sky to cooperate, so give it a couple of days.

## X2（実用・数字で不安を消す型）
> Quiet money tip for the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route: it's not one ticket. It's a chain of cable cars, buses and a ropeway, so the full crossing runs to five figures in yen and no rail pass covers it. If you only want the snow wall, go up from the Toyama side and back instead. 2026 fares broken down here:
> (link: en-alpine-cost.html)
> #JapanTravel

## X3（比較・選ばせる型）
> Kanazawa or Toyama? They're 20 minutes apart, so honestly — do both. But if you must pick: Kanazawa for the garden, gold leaf and geisha district; Toyama for mountains rising out of the sea and far fewer people. One is polished, one has room to breathe. Side-by-side (crowds, cost, English):
> (link: en-vs-kanazawa.html)

## X4（誤解を解く・逆張り実用型）
> Rain in Toyama isn't a write-off. Glass-art museum inside a Kengo Kuma building, a slow sushi lunch that doesn't care about weather, and a national-treasure Zen temple whose corridors look better in soft rain. Save the coast for your clear morning. What to actually do when it's wet:
> (link: en-rainy-day.html)
