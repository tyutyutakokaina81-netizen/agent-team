Not a promo, just something I find quietly moving as a local.

Most people know the Doraemon museum near Tokyo. Far fewer know the creator,
Fujiko F. Fujio, actually grew up in Takaoka, a small city on the Sea of Japan
— 15 minutes from Kanazawa by Shinkansen, no reservation, ~1,560 yen.

There's a free city park (Otogi-no-Mori) with statues of Doraemon, Nobita,
Shizuka, Gian and Suneo standing in a recreated "empty lot." On weekends the
strange part isn't the statues — it's the quiet line of foreign visitors,
often adults from Vietnam, India, China, Spain, waiting their turn to take a
photo. People standing where a piece of their childhood was born.

If you're doing Kanazawa and want a day-2 that isn't the packed Shirakawa-go
bus, this is an easy half-day: park, a pink "Anywhere Door" tram you can
actually ride, free bronze statues by the station, and a hometown gallery.
