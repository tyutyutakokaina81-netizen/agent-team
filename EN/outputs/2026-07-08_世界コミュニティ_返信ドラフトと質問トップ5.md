### D1. 「Is Toyama worth it / how many days?」型
> Toyama is genuinely worth a stop, and 2 nights is the sweet spot if you want more than just the Alpine Route. Day 1: Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route (it eats a full day — start early). Day 2: split between Takaoka (Zuiryu-ji temple, the Great Buddha, casting/copperware workshops) and Himi on the coast for the fish market and, on a clear morning, the Tateyama range floating over the sea. If you only have 1 night, skip the Alpine Route and do Takaoka + Himi — it's flatter, cheaper, and far less crowded than Kanazawa. Happy to answer specifics if you tell me your month (the Alpine Route is seasonal).

### D2. 「How to get to Toyama/Takaoka from Tokyo or Kanazawa?」型
> From Tokyo it's one Hokuriku Shinkansen ride to Toyama (~2h). From Kanazawa it's only ~20–25 min by Shinkansen, so Toyama/Takaoka make an easy add-on to a Kanazawa trip. For Takaoka specifically, get off at Shin-Takaoka (Shinkansen) or ride the local Ainokaze line from Toyama (~20 min). Himi is a short local train beyond Takaoka. If you have a JR Pass, the Shinkansen legs are covered; the Himi line is a cheap local add-on. Tell me your start point and I'll sketch the exact transfers.

### D3. 「Vegetarian/vegan in rural Japan — is it doable? dashi problem」型
> Honest answer: rural Japan is harder than Tokyo/Kyoto for strict veg, mainly because dashi (bonito/fish stock) hides in "vegetable" dishes. What actually works: (1) learn the phrase for "no fish stock / no meat" and show it written; (2) lean on shojin-ryori (Buddhist temple cuisine — genuinely vegan) where available; (3) kombu/shiitake-dashi dishes, tofu, and pickles are safe bets; (4) supermarkets and conbini have clearly-labeled options for self-catering. In Toyama specifically, temple areas and larger stations are your safest bases. It's very doable if you plan the dashi issue in advance rather than assuming "vegetable = veg."

### D4. 「Halal in rural Japan / Hokuriku?」型
> Halal-certified restaurants thin out fast once you leave the big cities, so in a place like Toyama the realistic play is (1) seafood + vegetable meals (sushi/sashimi without alcohol-based sauces, grilled fish), (2) self-catering from supermarkets where ingredients are labeled, and (3) checking the Japan Halal / prayer-space apps before you go, since coverage changes. Bigger stations and hotels are more likely to help with prayer space than small towns. I'm a local and happy to point you to which towns have more options — tell me your route and I'll be specific about what's realistic vs. what you should pre-pack.

### D5. 「Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route timing / snow wall / crowds」型
> Key timing facts: the Alpine Route opens mid-April and the famous "snow corridor" (Yuki-no-Otani) walls are tallest in April–May, usually shrinking through June. Autumn foliage (late Sept–Oct) is the other peak. It's a one-way traverse using multiple transport modes, so it takes most of a day — go as early as possible to beat both crowds and afternoon cloud. Book timed tickets in the snow-wall season; it sells out. If you're chasing the snow walls specifically, don't come after early June. Tell me your dates and I'll tell you what you'll realistically see.
