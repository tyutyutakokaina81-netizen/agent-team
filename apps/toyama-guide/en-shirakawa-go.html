Shirakawa-go & Gokayama from Toyama

The thatched gassho-zukuri farmhouses are one of the most photographed images of rural Japan — and Toyama is one of the best bases for reaching them. There are actually two World Heritage areas here: famous Shirakawa-go (just over the border in Gifu), and quieter Gokayama, which is in Toyama itself. A local's honest tip up front: if you want the postcard crowds, go to Shirakawa-go; if you want something calmer and more lived-in, Gokayama is the secret. Here's how to reach both, with the real bus details.

Gokayama — Toyama's own, and the quieter one

Gokayama has two thatched hamlets, Ainokura and Suganuma, and far fewer visitors than Shirakawa-go. It's reached by the Kaetsunou "World Heritage Bus" from Shin-Takaoka Station (on the Hokuriku Shinkansen) or Takaoka Station.

  • Journey: roughly 1 hour from Shin-Takaoka to Ainokura.
  • Fare: around ¥1,000–1,200 one way.
  • Good to pair with Takaoka and the Zuiryuji temple, since the bus starts from there.

Shirakawa-go — the famous one

Shirakawa-go is the big-name village (technically in Gifu Prefecture, but easy from Toyama). There's a direct highway bus from Toyama Station.

  • Journey: about 90 minutes direct.
  • Fare: around ¥2,400 one way; only a few departures a day.
  • Reserve ahead in busy seasons — book online through Japan Bus Online or the operator.

Want to see both? The combined ticket

If you'd like to do Gokayama and Shirakawa-go together, the "Gokayama–Shirakawago" ticket links Takaoka / Shin-Takaoka with Shirakawa-go via the Gokayama hamlets. It runs about ¥3,500 for adults (around ¥1,750 for children) and is typically valid for two days, which lets you stop at Zuiryuji, Ainokura, Suganuma and Shirakawa-go on one route. There are also one-day free passes covering the buses between Ainokura and Shirakawa-go. For most people, basing in Toyama or Takaoka and doing this as a long day (or an overnight) is the easiest way to tick off both.

Which should you choose?

  • Short on time, want the iconic view? Shirakawa-go.
  • Want it quiet, local and uncrowded? Gokayama (Ainokura).
  • Want the full experience? Both, with the two-day combined ticket.

Good to know before you go

  • Timetables and fares change by season — and the winter schedule differs. Always check the current Kaetsunou / Nouhi bus times before you travel; seat reservations on the local World Heritage Bus generally aren't possible, so allow buffer time.
  • The villages are small. A few hours in each is plenty; an overnight in a thatched farmhouse is the magic upgrade if you have time.
  • Base yourself well. Toyama City and Takaoka are the natural launch points — see where to stay.
