Toyama: a manga pilgrimage to two legends

Here's something most fans never realize: two of the most beloved names in manga history came from the same quiet stretch of the Sea of Japan coast, and grew up just a short train ride apart. Toyama is the birthplace of the duo who worked as "Fujiko Fujio" — the creator of Doraemon was from Takaoka, and the creator of Ninja Hattori-kun from neighbouring Himi. For anyone who grew up on these stories — and that's a huge slice of the world — Toyama is a genuinely moving, refreshingly uncommercial pilgrimage. Here's how to do it.

Two friends, two towns, one coast

The "Fujiko Fujio" name belonged to two childhood friends from Toyama. Fujiko F. Fujio (Hiroshi Fujimoto), creator of Doraemon, grew up in Takaoka. Fujiko Fujio Ⓐ (Motoo Abiko), creator of Ninja Hattori-kun, Kaibutsu-kun and The Laughing Salesman, was from Himi. They met as schoolboys, moved to Tokyo together, and changed manga forever. Tracing their roots here means visiting two real, lived-in towns — not a theme park — on the same easy line.

A suggested day

Both towns sit on the same coast, so you can comfortably do both in a day. A natural rhythm: start in Takaoka for the Doraemon touches plus its 400-year-old bronze-casting streets and Great Buddha; then ride the local JR Himi Line out to Himi for the Hattori-kun trail, a seafood lunch, and — on a clear day — the Tateyama mountains rising over the bay. More than manga

The beauty of this pilgrimage is that the towns are wonderful on their own terms. You'll get real Japanese craft (Takaoka), a working fishing port (Himi), one of Japan's great sea-and-mountain views at Amaharashi, and some of the best-value seafood on the coast. The characters are the reason you come; the place is the reason you stay.

How to get there & where to stay

Takaoka is about 15–40 minutes from Kanazawa, or roughly 2.5–3 hours from Tokyo via the Hokuriku Shinkansen to Shin-Takaoka; Himi is a short ride on from Takaoka. Base yourself in Toyama City or by the sea toward Himi, and you can do the whole pilgrimage at a relaxed pace.
