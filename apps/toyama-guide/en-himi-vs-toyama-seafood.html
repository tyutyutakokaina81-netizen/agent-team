Himi vs Toyama City for Seafood: Where to Go

I live in Takaoka, which sits between Himi and Toyama City. Both are close enough that I go back and forth for fish depending on my mood, so people often ask me which one they should pick for a seafood trip. The honest answer is that they are different in character, not simply better or worse. Here is how I actually think about it.

Himi: small port town, fish close to the boats

Himi is a fishing town on Toyama Bay. The scale is small, and that is the point. The port, the market area, and the restaurants are within a short distance of each other, so the whole town feels tied to the day's catch. When the boats come in during the colder months, the sense that the fish is local and recent is strong.

Himi is best known locally for winter yellowtail, which people here call *kanburi*. This is a seasonal thing, roughly late autumn into winter, and it can vary year to year with the catch. If you come in summer expecting the famous winter yellowtail, you may be disappointed, so match your timing to what you want to eat.

The trade-off is choice and access. Himi has fewer restaurants overall, some close early or take irregular days off, and the last useful trains back can be earlier than visitors expect. Getting there without a car takes a bit of planning. If you want a quiet, port-town day built around one or two good meals, Himi delivers that better than the city does.

Toyama City: more options, easier logistics

Toyama City is the prefectural capital, so it is a different experience. There are far more restaurants across price ranges, more sushi counters, and it is simply easier to arrive, eat, and find a room. The city is a normal transport hub, so trains and connections are more forgiving if your schedule is loose.

Toyama City is associated with white shrimp (*shiroebi*) and firefly squid (*hotaruika*), both of which have their own seasons — firefly squid is a spring thing in particular. You will see these on menus in the city, though whether any single dish is caught that day is not something a visitor can verify, so treat "local" claims with normal caution.

What you give up in the city is atmosphere. You are eating good seafood in a normal city setting, not beside the boats. The fish can be excellent, but the sense of standing in a working port is not there in the same way.

So which one?

  • Want the strongest port feeling and a specific seasonal fish, and you don't mind planning around trains and limited hours? Himi.
  • Want variety, more sushi options, easier arrival and lodging, or you're arriving late or on a tight schedule? Toyama City.
  • Have two days? Do both. From Takaoka in the middle, either is an easy day trip, and seeing the contrast is honestly the better trip.

One practical note: reservations help at the better places in both, especially in peak season. Many small spots don't take English calls, so asking your accommodation to book can save you a closed door.

Fact-check note

  • ✅ Himi is associated with winter yellowtail; Toyama City with white shrimp and firefly squid. Firefly squid is a spring season item. (Confirmed as regional association.)
  • ⚠️ Exact seasons and whether a given catch is landed depend on the year and weather — 諸説あります. Confirm current season before planning a trip around one fish.
  • ⚠️ Restaurant hours, days off, and last-train times change. Check current schedules yourself; the ones I know shift, so treat my timing warnings as directional, not exact.
  • ⚠️ "Caught today / local" claims on menus can't be verified by a visitor. I'm not able to confirm sourcing for any specific restaurant.
