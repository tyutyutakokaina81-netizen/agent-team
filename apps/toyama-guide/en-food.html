Food · Toyama, Japan

What to eat in Toyama — a local food guide

Toyama sits on one of Japan's richest fishing grounds, the deep, cold Toyama Bay, with the Northern Alps rising right behind it. The result is a quiet food paradise that most travelers miss. You don't need a famous restaurant here — even a modest counter near the station can serve fish that would cost a fortune in Tokyo. Here's what a local would tell you to order.

The essentials

  • White shrimp (shiroebi) — tiny, translucent, delicately sweet. Try it as sashimi or tempura. The "jewel of Toyama Bay."
  • Himi yellowtail (kanburi) — winter's prize: rich, fatty yellowtail from the port of Himi.
  • Kelp-cured sashimi (kobujime) — fish pressed with kombu kelp until the umami deepens. A Toyama home-cooking tradition.
  • Masuzushi — pressed trout sushi wrapped in bamboo leaf, sold in a round wooden case. The local taste varies shop to shop.
  • Toyama Black ramen — an intensely dark, soy-heavy ramen. Order rice on the side, like the locals do.
  • Firefly squid (hotaruika) — a spring specialty, glowing faintly blue; usually served boiled or marinated.

When it's in season

SeasonDon't miss

How to eat like a local

Keep it simple. Sweet, delicate fish like white shrimp wants light salt or a touch of kelp dashi more than heavy soy. With Toyama Black ramen, always get rice. And if you see kobujime on a menu, order it — it's the flavor that makes Toyama people homesick.

This is a general guide and does not guarantee any specific service or outcome. Seasons and availability vary by year and provider.
