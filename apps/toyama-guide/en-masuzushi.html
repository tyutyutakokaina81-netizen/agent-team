Masuzushi — Toyama's trout pressed sushi

Masuzushi is Toyama's best-known local sushi: vinegared trout pressed over a round of rice, wrapped in bamboo leaves and packed in a thin wooden hoop. It travels well, keeps for a day, and has long been a favorite train lunch (ekiben).

What it is

Unlike the nigiri you slice and eat at once, masuzushi is a pressed sushi: the fish and rice are packed together and weighted, then cut into wedges like a cake. The bamboo leaves aren't decoration — they add a faint aroma and help it keep.

Why Toyama

The dish grew up around the rivers and rail of Toyama, and became a classic station lunch sold across the region. Each shop guards its own balance of vinegar, salt and how lightly or firmly the trout is cured.

How to enjoy it

  • It's room-temperature food — perfect to carry onto a train or up to a viewpoint.
  • Try more than one shop; the differences in tartness and texture are real.
  • A wedge with tea makes an easy, very local lunch.

Where to find it
