Travel · Tateyama, Toyama

Shomyo Falls — Japan's tallest waterfall, off the Tateyama snow

Shomyo Falls, in the mountains of Toyama, drops about 350 m — the tallest waterfall in Japan. It is fed by snowmelt from the Tateyama range, so it is at its most powerful in spring and early summer. It sees a fraction of the crowds of Japan's famous sights.

How big is it?

At roughly 350 m, Shomyo is Japan's tallest waterfall by drop. In the snowmelt season a second cascade, Hannoki Falls, appears alongside it and is seasonally even taller — together they are a genuinely large sight, not a roadside trickle.

When to go

Late spring to early summer brings the strongest flow, as the Tateyama snow melts. Autumn adds color to the surrounding gorge. Deep-winter access is limited by snow, so check seasonal opening before you plan.

What to expect

From the access point it is a walk along the gorge to the viewing area; at full flow the spray and the sound are the whole point. Wear shoes you don't mind getting damp, and bring a layer — the mountain air stays cool even in summer.

How to get there

It sits on the Tateyama side of Toyama. Most travelers reach Toyama by Hokuriku Shinkansen, then continue by local train and bus toward the Tateyama area. It pairs naturally with the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route in season.
