Travel · Takaoka, Toyama

Zuiryuji — a National Treasure, without the crowds

Zuiryuji is a Zen temple in Takaoka whose main buildings are designated National Treasures of Japan. Built in the 17th century to honor the local lord, its calm, symmetrical layout still rewards a slow visit — and it sees far fewer visitors than the famous temples of Kyoto or Kamakura.

Why it matters

Several of its structures carry National Treasure or Important Cultural Property status. That puts it in rare company, yet on most days it is genuinely quiet.

What to look for

The appeal is order and proportion: a clean axial layout, heavy timber joinery, and broad gravel courts that change with the light. It is a place to walk slowly and notice craft, not to tick off a list.

Pairing it with the town

It sits close to the station and pairs naturally with the Takaoka Great Buddha and the old casting district, so you can see the temple, the bronze Buddha and a copperware showroom in one unhurried day.

How to get there

A short walk or local ride from Takaoka / Shin-Takaoka stations. Check current opening hours before you go, especially in winter.
