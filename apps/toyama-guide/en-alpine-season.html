When does the Alpine Route open?

Short version from someone who lives in Toyama: the Tateyama-Kurobe Alpine Route is a seasonal mountain crossing. It opens in spring — usually somewhere around the middle of April — and closes again in late autumn, around the end of November. The rest of the year it is shut under deep snow. Here is the part people get wrong: the exact opening day, the exact closing day, and the snow-wall period are set fresh every year by the operator, and they move with the weather. So treat every month I mention below as approximate, and confirm the real 2026 dates on the official Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route site before you fix flights or lodging.

The general shape of the season

In a normal year the route opens in spring — roughly mid-April is the pattern, though I would not put money on a specific day — and stays open until the closing in late autumn, around the end of November. Within that window the mountain changes a lot: early on, deep snow and cold air; by midsummer, green upper stations and much milder weather; then autumn colour before everything shuts. It is really several different trips depending on when you go, which is why I point people at when to go to Toyama first, then the the Alpine Route overview for how the crossing works.

When is the snow wall at its best?

The famous snow wall — the corridor of towering white you have seen in photos — is tallest and most reliable in the earlier part of the season, in the first weeks after opening. Think spring into early summer as the rough window. It does not last: as the weather warms the walls shrink steadily, so by deep summer the dramatic corridor is mostly gone. If the wall is your main reason for coming, aim for early in the open season — but again, confirm the operator's own snow-wall dates for 2026, because how long it stays impressive depends on that winter's snowfall and the spring melt. More on it on the snow wall page.

Why the exact dates actually matter

This is not a detail you can wave off. You may be booking flights, lodging and buses around a route that might not be fully open on the day you arrive, or might already be past its best snow. If you build a whole trip around a date the route turns out to be closed, there is no fixing it on the ground. So pin the operator's 2026 opening and closing dates before you commit to anything non-refundable. While you plan the money side, the Alpine Route costs page is worth a look, since the ticketing is its own small puzzle.

Shoulder timing, weather and the edges of the season

A couple of honest caveats. The very first and very last days of the season can run with limited sections rather than the full crossing, so an opening-day trip is not always the whole route — check what is actually running. Weather can also close parts of the route at short notice at any point in the season; the mountain does what it wants. And if you are coming in summer for water rather than snow, the discharge season at Kurobe Dam overlaps those warmer months, which can make a summer visit worthwhile even once the snow wall has shrunk.

The 2026 opening date, closing date and snow-wall period are decided by the operator and change with the year's weather. Look them up on the official Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route site, then book.
