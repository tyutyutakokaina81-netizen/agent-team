Coin Lockers & Luggage Storage at Toyama and Takaoka Stations

I live in Takaoka and pass through both stations most weeks. If you are arriving with a big suitcase and want to drop it before wandering, here is what I actually see on the ground — which lockers sit near which exits, what fits, and where a staffed counter can help when the lockers are full.

Toyama Station: lockers by size and exit

Toyama Station is the bigger of the two, and it has coin lockers in several clusters rather than one big wall. You will find them spread around the concourse that connects the JR gates, the Shinkansen gates, and the tram stop.

Three sizes are common here, and it helps to know them by what fits:

  • Small (about 30 cm deep): a daypack or a tote. Cheapest tier.
  • Medium: a carry-on up to roughly 55–60 cm, or a couple of backpacks.
  • Large: a full-size checked suitcase, the kind you check on a flight. There are noticeably fewer of these.

The large lockers are the ones that go first, especially late morning when day-trippers arrive. If you are coming off the Shinkansen with a big case, check the bank nearest the Shinkansen gates first, then walk toward the north side toward the tram — there is usually a second cluster there. I would not count on finding a large one free at midday on a weekend; that is the honest reality.

Most lockers here take coins, and many banks now also take IC cards (Suica, ICOCA and the like) or have a touch-panel payment unit. Carry a few ¥100 coins anyway, since not every unit is the newer type.

Takaoka Station: smaller, quieter, usually easier

Takaoka is my home station and it is calmer. The locker count is smaller, but so are the crowds, so your odds of finding a free medium or large locker are often better than at Toyama on a busy day.

The lockers sit near the main concourse that links the north and south sides of the station. If you come out toward the tram and the older shopping side (the south), the locker bank is a short walk from the ticket gates. Sizes follow the same small/medium/large pattern, though the number of large units is limited — if you have two big suitcases, assume you may only get one locker and plan around that.

Takaoka is also where you change for the Himi line and the tram toward the coast, so a lot of people stash a bag here for a few hours and come back. That works well for a half-day trip.

When the lockers are full: staffed storage

Coin lockers are not your only option, and this matters if you have oversized luggage or you strike out on a large locker.

  • Staffed baggage counters exist at or near the larger station. Hours and exact location change, so this is a ⚠️ please confirm on the day item — ask at the station information window, which is used to this question.
  • Same-day delivery / takuhaibin counters can forward a suitcase to a hotel or airport. This is genuinely useful if you are moving on and do not want to haul a case around Toyama or Takaoka. Fees and cut-off times vary by destination, so confirm at the counter.
  • Some hotels will hold your bag before check-in or after check-out even if you ask nicely on the day — worth a try if your room is nearby.

A practical tip: if you specifically need a large locker, arriving before mid-morning is your best window at Toyama. At Takaoka you usually have more slack.

Fact-check note
  • ✅ Both Toyama and Takaoka stations have coin lockers in small/medium/large sizes, with large ones being the least numerous.
  • ✅ Many lockers now accept IC-card payment as well as coins; carrying ¥100 coins is still sensible.
  • ⚠️ Exact locker counts, prices, and the precise position of each bank change over time — verify on arrival.
  • ⚠️ Staffed baggage-storage and takuhaibin counter hours and locations vary and should be confirmed at the station information window on the day.
  • Whether a specific full-size suitcase fits a given "large" locker can vary by a few centimetres — 諸説あります (accounts differ), so measure if your case is unusually tall.

Before you go

Where to stay: for overseas visitors, booking sites like Booking.com or Agoda are usually the easiest way to compare hotels near either station.
