Toyama travel budget — what 2–3 days actually costs

One of the quiet advantages of Toyama is that it's gentler on the wallet than the big-name cities — accommodation and food run a little cheaper than Tokyo or Kyoto, and most sights are modestly priced. The main expense for overseas visitors is simply getting here. Here's an honest, itemised look at what 2–3 days actually costs, broken into transport, where you sleep, food, tickets and local travel, with a few sample totals. All figures are approximate 2026 amounts in yen, per person unless noted, and prices change — treat them as planning guides, not quotes, and confirm current rates before you book.

Getting here — usually the biggest line item

For most international visitors the largest single cost is the rail journey in and out. From Tokyo, the Hokuriku Shinkansen (bullet train) reaches Toyama in roughly 2–2.5 hours.

  • Tokyo ↔ Toyama, Hokuriku Shinkansen: on the order of ¥13,000–16,000 one way for a reserved seat, so allow roughly ¥26,000–32,000 return. The exact fare depends on seat type and service.
  • From Kanazawa: about 20–30 minutes by Shinkansen, a much smaller fare — which is why Toyama works well bolted onto a Kanazawa trip or as a day trip.
  • Rail passes: often not worth it for a focused Toyama trip after recent price rises — add up your actual journeys first. See our honest breakdown of rail passes for Toyama.

If your trip is a wider Tokyo → Hokuriku → Osaka arc, the maths shifts; otherwise, plain tickets are usually cheaper. Either way, getting around once you're here is inexpensive — see getting around Toyama.

Where you stay — pick your band

Accommodation is where you most control the budget. Toyama is generally a little cheaper than the major cities, and prices swing with season — they tend to peak in spring (the Alpine Route opening) and around winter crab season, and ease off in quieter months. Rough per-night, per-room bands:

  • Business hotels (city base): roughly ¥8,000–15,000 per night for a clean single or compact double near Toyama Station — the easy, well-connected choice for most first visits.
  • Mid-range hotels: roughly ¥12,000–20,000 for a more comfortable room.
  • Seaside inns / ryokan with two meals (a splurge): commonly ¥15,000–40,000 per person, especially the seafood inns in Himi during crab and yellowtail season. This is a meal-plus-stay experience, not just a bed.

Food — easy to keep cheap, easy to splurge

Food in Toyama can be as budget or as indulgent as you like. The everyday end is genuinely cheap; the splurge end is the local seafood, which is the reason a lot of people come. Per person, per day:

  • Budget day (konbini breakfast, casual lunch, a simple dinner): roughly ¥3,000–4,000.
  • Comfortable day (sit-down meals, a sushi or local-speciality lunch): roughly ¥5,000–8,000.
  • One seafood splurge dinner: a generous sushi or crab/yellowtail meal can easily run ¥8,000+ — worth planning for at least once. See the local specialities in our Toyama food guide.

A fair planning figure for most travellers is around ¥4,000–6,000 a day on food, with extra set aside for one standout seafood meal.

Attractions and entry tickets — mostly modest

Most Toyama sights are inexpensive; the one genuinely premium experience is the mountain crossing.

  • City museums and sights: typically in the low hundreds to around ¥1,000–1,500 each — for example, the permanent collection at the Toyama Glass Art Museum is only a few hundred yen, with higher fees for special exhibitions.
  • Temples and gardens: often free or a few hundred yen.
  • The Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route: the big exception — a spectacular but pricey ride. The full crossing's transport alone runs into five figures per person. Budget it separately; full numbers are in Alpine Route costs.

For a city-and-coast trip without the Alpine Route, allow only modest sums for entry tickets — perhaps ¥2,000–4,000 across two or three days. Add the Alpine Route on top if you're doing it.

Getting around locally — cheap and simple

Local transport barely dents the budget. The city's retro trams run a flat fare of a couple of hundred yen per ride, and a city tram-and-bus day pass is inexpensive if you're hopping around. Short local-rail hops out to Takaoka, Himi and the Amaharashi Coast are well under an hour and modestly priced. As a planning figure, ¥1,000–2,000 a day covers most people's local trams, buses and short train hops. Most visitors don't need a car — see getting around Toyama for the honest take.

Sample totals for 2–3 days

Putting it together — per person, excluding the Shinkansen to and from Toyama, and assuming you're not crossing the Alpine Route (add that separately):

  • Budget, 2 nights / 3 days: roughly ¥25,000–35,000 (business-hotel base, mostly casual food, trams and short rail hops).
  • Comfortable, 2 nights / 3 days: roughly ¥45,000–65,000 (mid-range or one seaside-inn night, sit-down meals including a seafood dinner, the same easy local transport).

Add the return Shinkansen (~¥26,000–32,000 from Tokyo) on top, plus the Alpine Route if you ride it. These are illustrative ranges, not fixed prices — your real total depends on season, accommodation choice and how much you lean into the seafood.
