Food · Toyama

Toyama crab — red snow crab, and the winter table

Toyama Bay is deep and cold, and that makes it good crab country. The local star is the red snow crab (benizuwai), branded locally as 'Koshi no Akagani' — sweeter and more affordable than the famous high-end zuwai, and a fixture of the winter table here.

What kind of crab?

The everyday Toyama crab is the red snow crab (benizuwai), landed from the deep bay. It is delicate and sweet, often sold boiled and ready to eat. The pricier zuwai (snow crab) also appears in season; benizuwai is the one locals reach for more often.

When to go

Crab is a cold-season pleasure — roughly autumn into late winter, depending on the fishery's opening. Exact dates shift each year, so check the latest before you plan a crab trip.

How locals enjoy it

  • Boiled, as is — the simplest way to taste the sweetness.
  • Sashimi — very fresh, in season.
  • Hot pot (nabe) — a winter staple, finished with rice porridge.
