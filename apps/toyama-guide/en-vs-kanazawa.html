Toyama or Kanazawa? Which to visit — and how to do both

If you're planning the Sea of Japan side of central Japan, you've probably landed on the same question many travelers do: Toyama or Kanazawa? The honest answer is that they're very different in character — and, because they're barely 20 minutes apart by train, you don't really have to choose. Here's a local's straight comparison to help you decide where to spend your time.

The short answer

Kanazawa is the polished, famous one: a celebrated garden, preserved geisha and samurai districts, gold leaf, and excellent museums. Toyama is the quieter, more local one: arguably even better seafood, mountains that rise straight out of the sea, craft towns, and a fraction of the crowds. Kanazawa is the headline; Toyama is the place you tell your friends about afterwards.

Choose Kanazawa if…

…you want a compact, classic Japanese city experience: famous gardens, atmospheric old streets, museums and refined food, all walkable. It's a superb first stop on this coast and deserves its reputation.

Choose Toyama if…

…you'd rather trade polish for authenticity: some of Japan's best-value seafood, dramatic sea-and-mountain scenery, real craft towns like Takaoka and the fishing port of Himi, and the quiet thrill of being somewhere most foreign tourists skip. If you're a manga or anime fan, Toyama also wins outright as the home of two legendary creators.

Honestly? Do both

This is the move most people miss. Kanazawa and Toyama are about 20–25 minutes apart on the train, so you can base yourself in either and day-trip to the other. A classic combination: a couple of days in Kanazawa for the highlights, then a day in Toyama for the food, the coast and the calm. See how to get around for the practical details.

Where to stay

If Toyama is in your plan, basing yourself in Toyama City gives you easy reach of both the coast and the mountains; a seaside inn toward Himi is the scenic alternative.

A quieter, often cheaper base

One practical reason to lean toward Toyama isn't scenery at all — it's using it as your base. As the less-touristed city, Toyama lodging tends to be cheaper and the city quieter than Kanazawa, especially in peak season, though this is a general tendency rather than a guarantee and rates move around a lot. Because the two cities are only a short train hop apart, basing yourself in Toyama and day-tripping into Kanazawa is easy to combine — just confirm current times and fares officially before you rely on them.

If that appeals, it pairs naturally with a 7-day route based in Toyama for a wider loop, or, coming the other way, a Kanazawa→Takaoka day trip if Kanazawa is where you've already booked your nights.
