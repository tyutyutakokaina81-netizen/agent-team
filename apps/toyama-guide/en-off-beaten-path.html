Off the beaten path in Japan: 7 reasons to choose Toyama

Kyoto is magical — and, these days, mobbed. If you're after the Japan behind the postcards, where you can still feel like you've discovered something, point yourself at the Sea of Japan coast. Here are seven honest reasons a local thinks Toyama is one of the country's most rewarding under-the-radar regions — and barely a foreign tourist in sight.

1. The Alps literally rise out of the sea

On a clear morning at Amaharashi or Himi, 3,000-metre snow peaks appear to grow straight out of the bay. There are only a handful of places on Earth like it, and almost no one photographs it.

2. Possibly the best-value seafood in Japan

Toyama Bay is one of the country's richest fishing grounds. White shrimp, winter yellowtail, snow crab, kelp-cured sashimi — even a modest counter serves fish that would cost a fortune in Tokyo. See the food guide.

3. A 400-year-old craft town

Takaoka has been casting bronze for four centuries. Walk its old metal-casters' streets and you're in a working craft city, not a museum.

4. Snow villages without the crowds

Everyone queues for Shirakawa-go. The UNESCO thatched villages of Gokayama are just as beautiful and far quieter.

5. One of Japan's great mountain journeys

The Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route climbs over the Northern Alps by cable car, ropeway and bus — with towering walls of spring snow.

6. It's a manga pilgrimage

Toyama is the birthplace of the creators of Doraemon and Ninja Hattori-kun. Two beloved legends, two small towns, one short train line. See the pilgrimage guide.

7. It's absurdly easy to reach

For somewhere this under-visited, it's remarkably accessible: about 2 hours from Tokyo by bullet train, and just 20 minutes from Kanazawa — so you can add it without backtracking. See the day trip or how many days you need.

So, is Toyama worth it?

If you want temples-and-crowds Japan, stay on the Golden Route. If you want a quieter, more local, more delicious Japan — with mountains, sea and real craft — Toyama is one of the best bets in the country. Stay a night or two and see for yourself.
