Toyama, Japan — a local-eyes guide to Takaoka & Himi

Toyama sits on the Sea of Japan, between Kanazawa and the Northern Alps. It's not a big tourist machine — which is the point. Here's what's actually worth your time: the food, how to get here, and where to stay. Written from the ground, without the hype.

Why Toyama (and why Takaoka / Himi)

Most travelers rush between Kanazawa and the Alpine Route and skip the coast. But Toyama Bay is one of Japan's richest fishing grounds, and the smaller cities — Takaoka (400 years of metal craft, and the hometown of Doraemon's creator) and Himi (a working fishing port) — give you a quieter, more local side of Japan.

What to eat

  • White shrimp (shiroebi) — tiny, delicately sweet shrimp from Toyama Bay. Try it as sashimi or in tempura (kakiage).
  • Himi seafood — the port is famous for winter yellowtail (buri), but the white fish across seasons is excellent too.
  • Kombu-jime — sashimi pressed with kelp, a Toyama specialty that deepens the umami.
  • Local sushi — with this much fresh fish, even modest sushi spots punch above their weight.

How to get there

Toyama is on the Hokuriku Shinkansen — roughly 2 hours from Tokyo, and a short hop from Kanazawa. Takaoka and Himi are reachable by local train from Toyama or Shin-Takaoka. A rail pass can make sense if you're combining it with Tokyo / Kanazawa / the Alps.

Where to stay

Toyama city is the easiest base (good train access). Himi has seaside inns known for winter crab and yellowtail dinners; Takaoka works if you want a quieter, craft-town feel. Book ahead in winter (crab season) and during the Alpine Route season.

Things to do

  • Takaoka — historic metal-casting district, the Great Buddha, and Doraemon touches around town.
  • Himi — the seaside town and port; on clear winter days you can see the Tateyama mountains rising over the bay.
  • Day trips — Gokayama's thatched villages, and the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route in season.
