Toyama itinerary: how to spend 2–3 days

Toyama packs a lot into a small area — high mountains, a rich bay, craft towns and pop-culture history, all within an easy train ride of each other. That makes it ideal for a focused 2–3 day trip. Here's a flexible, local's plan you can follow or mix and match, with real travel logic and where to base yourself. Base in Toyama City and you can do all of it without changing hotels.

Day 1 — The coast, craft & the manga trail

Start in Takaoka: walk the 400-year-old metal-casting streets of Kanayamachi, see the Great Buddha, and catch the gentle Doraemon touches (the creator's hometown). Then ride the local JR Himi Line out to Himi for a seafood lunch and the Ninja Hattori-kun trail. On a clear day, stop at Amaharashi Coast to see the Alps rising out of the sea. It's a perfect mix of culture, sea and scenery — and the heart of the two-creator manga pilgrimage.

Day 2 — The mountains

If it's spring to autumn, give a full day to the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route — the snow corridor in spring, alpine air in summer, fiery colour in autumn. If the route is closed (winter) or you prefer something gentler, swap in the UNESCO thatched villages of Gokayama instead. Either way, this is your big-scenery day.

Day 3 — City, glass & food

Slow down in Toyama City: the Kengo Kuma–designed glass art museum, the canal park with one of Japan's prettiest cafés, and retro trams. Eat well — white shrimp, sushi, kelp-cured fish (see the food guide). It's the relaxed bookend to two active days.
