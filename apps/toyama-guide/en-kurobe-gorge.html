Travel · Kurobe, Toyama

Kurobe Gorge — the little Torokko train through a deep ravine

Kurobe Gorge is one of the deepest ravines in Japan, and the gentlest way to see it is the Torokko — a small, open-car scenic railway that was originally built for dam construction. It is a seasonal ride, at its best in fresh green and in autumn color.

The Torokko train

The open carriages run slowly along the gorge, crossing bridges and threading tunnels above the blue-green Kurobe River. It is unhurried, scenic, and easy — more a moving viewpoint than a thrill ride. Enclosed carriages are available if the weather turns.

When to go

The railway runs seasonally (roughly spring through autumn) and closes in winter; exact dates shift each year with snow, so check the latest operating schedule before planning. Autumn color is the headline season and books up.

Nearby

The gateway is the hot-spring town of Unazuki, an easy place to soak after the ride. It also pairs with the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route and Shomyo Falls for a mountains-and-water few days.

How to get there

Reach Toyama by Hokuriku Shinkansen, then continue by local railway toward Unazuki on the eastern side of the prefecture.
