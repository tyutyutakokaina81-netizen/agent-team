Toyama City — glass, a canal park, and the perfect base

Most people pass through Toyama City on the bullet train without stopping. That's a missed chance. It's a clean, walkable, surprisingly stylish city on the Sea of Japan — with a world-class glass art scene, a canal park that holds one of the prettiest cafés in Japan, and retro trams that make getting around half the fun. Best of all, it's the ideal base for everything around it: the bay, the craft town of Takaoka, the fishing port of Himi, and the high mountains. Here's how a local would use it.

Why base yourself here

Toyama City sits on the Hokuriku Shinkansen (about 2 hours from Tokyo), with easy local trains and trams fanning out in every direction. Stay here and you can do a mountain day and a coast day without changing hotels. It's also relaxed and affordable compared with the bigger tourist cities — you get good food and calm streets without the crowds.

Things to do

  • Toyama Glass Art Museum — Toyama has long been a "city of glass," and its museum sits inside a striking building designed by architect Kengo Kuma, with a soaring wooden atrium. Worth it even if you're not usually a museum person.
  • Fugan Unga Kansui Park — a graceful canal park, home to a café often called one of the most beautiful in Japan, especially at sunset.
  • Ride the trams — the city's retro and modern trams are a cheap, scenic way to get around, including a loop through the centre.
  • Eat from the bay — white shrimp, sushi, kelp-cured fish; even counters near the station serve seafood that punches far above its price.

Use it as a hub

This is the real reason to stay. From Toyama City you can reach Takaoka (bronze craft, the Great Buddha) and Himi (a fishing port where the Alps rise over the sea) in well under an hour, take the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route up into the mountains, or make the easy day trip loop if you're coming from Kanazawa. Mountains one day, sea the next — all from one base.

Where to stay

Hotels cluster conveniently around Toyama Station, which makes early starts for the mountains or the coast painless. Book ahead in the spring Alpine Route season and in winter (crab season), when rooms fill up.

When to go

Spring and autumn are mild and easy; spring also opens the Alpine Route. Winter brings crab and yellowtail (and snow). Summer is green and good for escaping the heat in the nearby mountains. The city works year-round as a comfortable, well-connected base.
