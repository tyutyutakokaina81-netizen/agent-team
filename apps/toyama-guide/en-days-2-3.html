Toyama Day 2 & Day 3 — detailed plans

You've done Day 1 — the coast, Takaoka and Himi (see the day-one loop). Now you've wisely decided to stay longer. Here are detailed, hour-by-hour plans for Day 2 and Day 3, written for an overseas visitor basing in Toyama City. They're flexible — swap pieces to suit the weather and the season — but this is the rhythm a local would suggest to make the most of a multi-night trip.

Day 2 — into the mountains

Toyama's second day is all about altitude. Choose the option that fits the season; both are unforgettable.

Option A (spring–autumn): the Alpine Route

TimePlan
Early AMLocal train from Toyama to the foot of the Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route. Reserve tickets ahead.
Late AMRide up by cable car and ropeway to Murodo (~2,450m). In spring, walk the famous "snow corridor."
LunchA simple meal at altitude, with alpine views.
AfternoonEasy high-altitude walks (ponds, viewpoints), then descend the same way back to Toyama.
EveningBack in the city for dinner — or move to an onsen inn for the night (highly recommended).

Option B (winter, or a gentler day): gorge & villages

If the high route is closed or you'd prefer something easier, ride the scenic Kurobe Gorge trolley train (warmer months) or visit the UNESCO thatched villages of Gokayama — quiet, storybook, and stunning under snow. Either pairs beautifully with an onsen evening.

Day 3 — city, glass, onsen & food

After a big mountain day, Day 3 slows the pace right down — and leans into the things that make people fall for Toyama.

TimePlan
MorningThe Toyama Glass Art Museum (a striking Kengo Kuma building) and a stroll through Toyama City by tram.
MiddayA seafood lunch — white shrimp, sushi, kelp-cured fish. See the food guide.
AfternoonFugan Unga Kansui Park and its famous canal-side café; or, if you missed it, the Doraemon & Hattori-kun trail.
Late afternoonA final soak — a city onsen, or check into a hot-spring inn if you're staying another night.
EveningA relaxed last dinner; pick up kelp and shrimp crackers as souvenirs.

Staying a 4th night?

If you have more time, branch out: a night near Kanazawa (20 minutes away) for gardens and old districts, or push toward Takayama and the Alps. See how many days you need and Toyama or Kanazawa to plan the wider trip.

Where to stay for Day 2 & 3

Keep Toyama City as your easy base, and move one night to an onsen or seaside inn for variety — that's the most restful way to do a multi-night trip. Book multi-night and onsen stays ahead, especially in spring and winter.
