Travel · Toyama City

Toyama glass — a modern craft, and a museum worth the stop

Beyond seafood and mountains, Toyama City has quietly become a center for contemporary glass art. It makes an easy, indoor half-day — especially handy on a rainy or very cold day — anchored by a striking modern museum in the center of town.

Why glass, here?

The city has invested in glass as a modern craft for decades, building studios, a school and public collections. The result is a small but real scene of contemporary glassmaking you can actually see and, in places, buy.

The Glass Art Museum

The Toyama Glass Art Museum sits in a bold modern building in the city center (shared with the public library), with a calm wood-and-light interior and rotating glass exhibitions. It is central, easy to reach, and a good rainy-day option.

Make a half-day of it

Pair it with the city's tram ride and its well-known canal-side park — see Toyama City for how to spend a base day here.

Getting there

Central Toyama is walkable and tram-connected from Toyama Station (Hokuriku Shinkansen).
