Tateyama Alpine Route: costs & how to do it

The Tateyama–Kurobe Alpine Route is one of Japan's most spectacular mountain journeys — and, honestly, one of its more expensive day trips. It's worth it, but you should walk in knowing the real numbers, the season, and how to plan so you don't get caught out. Here's the practical, honest version for 2026, from the Toyama side.

When it's open (2026)

  • Season: the route is open 15 April – 30 November 2026. It is closed in deep winter.
  • The snow wall (Yuki-no-Otani): the towering snow-corridor walk runs roughly 15 April – 25 June 2026. That's the window if the snow wall is your reason for coming.
  • Autumn colour on the heights arrives from late September; see when to go.

What it costs — be ready, it's not cheap

This is the part people underestimate. The Alpine Route is a chain of separate mountain transports, and the fares add up fast:

  • Through transport, Tateyama → Ogizawa (the full crossing): about ¥10,940 one way for an adult (2026).
  • From Toyama Station to Shinano-Omachi (including the local trains either end): about ¥14,010 one way.
  • Going up and back to the Toyama side (e.g. just to Murodo for the snow wall and return) is a different, often cheaper total than crossing all the way — see the tip below.
  • Luggage forwarding Toyama ↔ Shinano-Omachi / Omachi Onsen: about ¥4,000 per bag (available roughly mid-April to early November).
  • Wider area passes exist (e.g. the Alpine-Takayama-Matsumoto area pass, around ¥24,500) — only worth it if you're also covering Takayama and Matsumoto.

In short: budget for it as a premium experience, not a cheap local outing. For a couple crossing the whole route, the transport alone is a real chunk of a day's spending.

The sections you ride (Toyama side → Nagano)

You don't drive any of this — you switch between cable cars, a highland bus, a ropeway and tunnel buses. From the Toyama side it runs: Tateyama → Bijodaira (cable car) → Murodo (highland bus; the snow wall and the highest point, around 2,450m) → Daikanbo (tunnel electric bus, ~10 min, ¥2,200) → Kurobedaira (ropeway, ~7 min, ¥1,700) → Kurobeko (cable car, ~5 min, ¥1,150) → walk across the Kurobe Dam crest (~15 min, free) → Ogizawa (tunnel electric bus, ~16 min, ¥1,800) on the Nagano side. Done as a full crossing with sightseeing, allow 6–8 hours.

How to book

  • Reserve in peak season. Spring (mid-April to May, snow-wall time) is busiest and sells out. Web tickets typically go on sale around two months ahead.
  • Start early. It's a long day with several transfers; a first-thing start avoids queues at the cable car.
  • Decide your direction. A full crossing to Nagano suits people moving on to Matsumoto; if you're staying in Toyama, an out-and-back is simpler.

An honest money-saving tip

If the snow wall is the only thing you came for, you don't need to pay for the whole expensive crossing. From the Toyama side you can go up to Murodo, walk the snow corridor, and come back the way you came — that gives you the headline experience without the full Tateyama-to-Ogizawa fare. Save the complete crossing for when you're actually continuing through to Nagano.

Either way, an early start is far easier with a base near the mountain end. See where to stay in Toyama.

No Japan Rail Pass covers this — plan for it separately

This one catches a lot of people out. The Alpine Route is run by non-JR mountain operators (the cable cars, buses and ropeway aren't Japan Railways), so no Japan Rail Pass covers it — the nationwide JR Pass and regional passes like the Hokuriku Arch Pass don't include any of these sections. You pay for the route on top of whatever rail pass you hold. If you're weighing passes for getting to Toyama, see the Hokuriku Arch Pass explained.

Fares are charged by section, and there are one-way (full traverse) and round-trip ticket options — a return to Murodo and back is a different total from crossing all the way to Ogizawa. Prices vary by how far you go and by year, so check the official Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route site for current fares before you book.

This is a general guide. Operating dates, the snow-wall period, fares, luggage fees and pass prices are set by the route operators, vary by year and weather, and change without notice — the 2026 figures above are approximate and should be confirmed on the official Tateyama Kurobe Alpine Route site before you book.
