Travel · Takaoka, Toyama

Takaoka — Doraemon's hometown, 400 years of metalwork, and the sea

Most visitors to central Japan rush between Kanazawa and the Alpine Route and never stop in Takaoka. That's a quiet little mistake. Takaoka is a working craft city on the Sea of Japan — the birthplace of Fujiko F. Fujio (the creator of Doraemon), home to 400 years of bronze casting, and a short hop from the fishing town of Himi and the thatched villages of Gokayama. Here's what's actually worth your time, written from the ground.

Why Takaoka

Takaoka isn't a polished tourist machine, and that's the appeal. You get a real, lived-in Japanese city: old merchant streets, the smell of metal workshops, a giant bronze Buddha, and locals who are quietly surprised — and pleased — to see a traveler who bothered to get off the main line.

Things to do

  • Kanayamachi district — historic cobbled streets lined with the lattice-fronted houses of metal casters. The craft that built the city is still alive here.
  • The Great Buddha of Takaoka (Takaoka Daibutsu) — one of Japan's notable great Buddhas, cast in the city's signature bronze.
  • Zuiryuji Temple — a beautifully proportioned Zen temple, calm and uncrowded.
  • Doraemon touches — the beloved character's creator was born here, and you'll find gentle nods around town, including the Doraemon tram.
  • Day trip to Himi — a real fishing port; on clear winter mornings the Tateyama mountains rise straight out of the sea.
  • Day trip to Gokayama — UNESCO-listed thatched-roof villages, quieter than the famous Shirakawa-go.

What to eat

You're on Toyama Bay, one of Japan's richest fishing grounds. Don't miss white shrimp (shiroebi), the delicately sweet "jewel of the bay," and in winter, Himi yellowtail (buri). Even modest sushi counters here punch far above their price.

How to get there

Takaoka is on the Hokuriku Shinkansen line (via Shin-Takaoka), roughly 2.5–3 hours from Tokyo and a short ride from Kanazawa. A rail pass can pay off if you're combining it with Tokyo, Kanazawa and the Alps.

When to go

Winter brings crab and yellowtail (and dramatic mountain-over-sea views), spring brings firefly squid and cherry blossoms, and the green months are quiet and easy. Book ahead in crab season and during the Alpine Route season.
