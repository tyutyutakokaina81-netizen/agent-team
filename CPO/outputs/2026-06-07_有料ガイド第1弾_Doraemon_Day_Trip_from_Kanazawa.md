## EN

**Everyone staying in Kanazawa spends day two the same way — a packed bus to Shirakawa-go. Fifteen minutes away by Shinkansen is the place almost no foreign traveler knows: the real hometown of Doraemon.**

Not the museum near Tokyo. The hometown. Fujiko F. Fujio — the artist who drew the blue robot cat that half of Asia grew up on — actually grew up in Takaoka, a small city on the Sea of Japan coast, fifteen minutes from Kanazawa by bullet train.

This guide takes the day most travelers waste in a crowd and turns it into something quieter, stranger and far more personal. Here is what you can do there, even for free:

**1. Otogi-no-Mori Park — the five characters together.**
On an open lawn stand Doraemon, Nobita, Shizuka, Gian and Suneo, arranged like the empty lot from the series. Entry is free. You walk right up to them.

**2. The Doraemon Tram — riding through the "Anywhere Door."**
On the local Manyo Line runs a two-car tram wrapped inside and out with the characters. The boarding door is pink, shaped like the famous "Anywhere Door." It isn't a theme-park ride — it's an ordinary commuter tram that happens to be covered in your childhood. More than a million people have ridden it.

**3. Twelve bronze statues by the station.**
A free walking street outside Takaoka Station holds twelve bronze characters, open 24 hours, no ticket.

**A note from someone who lives nearby:** On a normal weekend at Otogi-no-Mori, the strange sight isn't the statues — it's the quiet line of foreign visitors waiting their turn to photograph them. Grown adults, phones ready, a little shy, standing where a piece of their childhood was actually born.

## 2. Access Details / アクセス詳細

### Kanazawa → Takaoka / 金沢 → 高岡
- **EN:** Hokuriku Shinkansen, Kanazawa → Shin-Takaoka, about **15 minutes**, around **¥1,560** (non-reserved). Roughly **2 services per hour**. Covered by the **JR Pass** and the Hokuriku Arch Pass. No seat reservation needed for non-reserved.
- **JA:** 北陸新幹線で金沢→新高岡、**約15分**、自由席で**¥1,560前後**。**1時間に約2本**。**JRパス**・北陸アーチパス対象。自由席なら座席予約不要。

### Shin-Takaoka ↔ Takaoka Station / 新高岡 ⇄ 高岡駅
- **EN:** Shin-Takaoka (Shinkansen) and Takaoka Station (city center, trams & buses) are a short hop apart — one stop on the JR Johana Line, or a few minutes by taxi/bus. Note these are **two different stations**.
- **JA:** 新高岡（新幹線）と高岡駅（市街地・トラム/バスの拠点）は別駅。JR城端線で1駅、またはタクシー/バスで数分。**2つは別の駅**である点に注意。

### To Otogi-no-Mori Park / おとぎの森公園へ
- **EN:** About a **15-minute walk** from Shin-Takaoka Station. **Free entry.** Open park — the statues are outdoors on the lawn.
- **JA:** 新高岡駅から**徒歩約15分**。**入園無料**。屋外の公園で、像は広場にある。

### Doraemon Tram (Manyo Line) / ドラえもんトラム（万葉線）
- **EN:** Board on the **Manyo Line** from Takaoka Station. Two-car tram, wrapped inside and out, pink "Anywhere Door." Normal fare, no reservation. ⚠️ Runs on **selected services only** — check the Manyo Line's operating info for the day before you go.
- **JA:** **高岡駅**から万葉線に乗車。2両編成・内外装飾・ピンクの「どこでもドア」扉。通常運賃・予約不要。⚠️**特定便のみ運行**の場合あり——乗車前に万葉線の運行情報で当日を確認。

### Takaoka → Gokayama / 高岡 → 五箇山
- **EN:** **World Heritage Bus** from Takaoka Station to Gokayama (Ainokura, Suganuma) — and on to Shirakawa-go if you ever want it. ⚠️ Only a few departures per day; check the current timetable and fares (a free pass / day ticket may save money).
- **JA:** 高岡駅から**世界遺産バス**で五箇山（相倉・菅沼）へ。さらに白川郷へも行ける。⚠️1日数便のみ。最新の時刻・運賃を確認（フリーきっぷ/1日券がお得な場合あり）。

## 3. Best Photo Angles / 写真ベストアングル

*(Most foreign visitors come for one reason: the photo. Here is how to get the shots that actually travel home.)*
*(多くの外国人の目的は撮影。ちゃんと「持ち帰れる一枚」の撮り方。)*

**1. All five characters in one frame — Otogi-no-Mori.**
EN: Shoot **straight on, from the front of the lawn**, low and slightly back, so Doraemon, Nobita, Shizuka, Gian and Suneo all line up in a single horizontal frame — the "empty lot" composition fans recognize instantly.
JA: 広場の**正面・やや低く・少し引いて**撮ると、5体が横一列に収まる。ファンが一目で分かる「空き地」構図。

**2. The pink "Anywhere Door" — the Tram.**
EN: Make the **pink boarding door the subject**. The surprise (an ordinary commuter tram covered in Doraemon) is the story — include a sliver of the everyday platform for contrast.
JA: **ピンクの乗車扉を主役**に。意外性（ふつうの路面電車がドラえもん）が物語。日常のホームを少し入れると対比が効く。

**3. The station bronzes — Takaoka Station walk.**
EN: 24-hour free street, so **early morning or evening light** gives clean shots with no crowds. One character close, the station soft behind.
JA: 24時間無料の通りなので、**早朝か夕方の光**で人を入れず撮れる。1体を寄りで、駅を背景にぼかす。

**4. Gokayama (Itinerary B).**
EN: The **Ainokura viewpoint** above the village gives the classic gassho-roofs-and-hills frame — the same look as Shirakawa-go, with fewer people in it.
JA: **相倉の展望スポット**から、合掌屋根＋山並みの定番構図。白川郷と同じ絵で、人が少ない。

## 5. FAQ

**Q1. Is half a day really enough? / 半日で足りる?**
EN: Yes. Statues + tram + station bronzes fit comfortably in a morning, and you're back in Kanazawa for the afternoon (Itinerary A). Add Gokayama only if you want a full day.
JA: 足りる。像＋トラム＋駅前銅像は午前で無理なく回れ、午後は金沢に戻れる（行程A）。1日かけたい人だけ五箇山を足す。

**Q2. What about a rainy day? / 雨の日は?**
EN: The statues and station bronzes are outdoors, but the Doraemon Tram and the Furusato Gallery (indoor, at the Takaoka Art Museum) are great rain options. A rainy half-day works fine.
JA: 像と駅前銅像は屋外だが、ドラえもんトラムとふるさとギャラリー（高岡市美術館2階・屋内）は雨向き。雨の半日でも成立する。

**Q3. Good with kids? / 子連れでも大丈夫?**
EN: Very. Otogi-no-Mori is an open, free park, the tram ride is short and fun, and walking distances are manageable. Itinerary A is the easy one for families.
JA: 向いている。おとぎの森は無料の広い公園、トラムは短くて楽しく、徒歩距離も無理がない。家族は行程Aが楽。

**Q4. Will English work? / 英語は通じる?**
EN: This is a small regional city, so English is limited outside major facilities. Download offline maps, keep station names handy, and a translation app helps. The trip itself is simple to navigate.
JA: 地方都市なので、主要施設以外で英語は限定的。オフライン地図・駅名メモ・翻訳アプリがあると安心。移動自体はシンプル。

**Q5. Can I combine this with Shirakawa-go? / 白川郷と組める?**
EN: The same World Heritage Bus line continues toward Shirakawa-go, so it's possible — but a Doraemon + Gokayama day (Itinerary B) is the quieter, more distinctive choice this guide recommends.
JA: 世界遺産バスは白川郷方面へも続くので可能。ただし本ガイドの推しは、より静かで個性的な「ドラえもん＋五箇山」（行程B）。
