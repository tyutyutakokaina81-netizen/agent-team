Page 3 — How to Use This Planner
- Step 1: Decide your base — the Fuji Five Lakes side (closer Fuji views) or the Hakone side (hot springs + a scenic loop). Many travelers do one each night.
- Step 2: Lock your transport first (Tokyo → Fuji/Hakone), then build the day around it.
- Step 3: Check the weather page early — Fuji shows itself best on clear, crisp mornings, so plan your view-chasing for then.
- Tip: Early morning and late afternoon usually give the clearest skies and softest light for Fuji.

Page 4 — Area Overview
- Fuji Five Lakes side: a cluster of lakes at the foot of Mt. Fuji, known for reflected-Fuji photos, lakeside walks, and viewpoints.
- Hakone side: a mountain hot-spring region reached by train and bus, famous for its open-air art, a scenic loop (train, switchback, ropeway, cruise), and relaxing baths.
- Getting around: from Tokyo, both areas are reachable by train and/or highway bus in roughly a couple of hours.
- Typical split: one day chasing Fuji views by the lakes, one day relaxing on the Hakone loop.
