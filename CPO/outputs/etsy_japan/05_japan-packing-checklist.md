### 1. Documents & Money
- [ ] Passport (valid 6+ months)
- [ ] Visa or entry permit (if required for your nationality)
- [ ] Printed flight / e-ticket confirmation
- [ ] Hotel / accommodation bookings
- [ ] Travel insurance card and policy number
- [ ] Driver's license + International Driving Permit (if renting a car)
- [ ] Credit / debit cards (tell your bank you're traveling)
- [ ] Some Japanese yen in cash (many small shops are cash-only)
- [ ] Coin purse (Japan uses a lot of coins)
- [ ] Emergency contact list on paper
- [ ] Photocopies / phone photos of passport and cards

### 2. Electronics
- [ ] Phone + charging cable
- [ ] Power bank (carry-on only, not checked luggage)
- [ ] Plug adapter (Japan uses Type A two flat pins, 100V)
- [ ] Camera + memory card + charger
- [ ] Earphones / headphones
- [ ] Pocket Wi-Fi or travel SIM / eSIM
- [ ] Multi-port USB charger
- [ ] E-reader / tablet
- [ ] Watch or fitness tracker + charger

### 3. Health & Toiletries
- [ ] Prescription medicine + copy of prescription
- [ ] Pain relief, motion-sickness, stomach, and cold tablets
- [ ] Hand sanitizer and pocket tissues (some restrooms lack both)
- [ ] Small towel / handkerchief (handy in public restrooms)
- [ ] Toothbrush, toothpaste, floss
- [ ] Deodorant
- [ ] Sunscreen
- [ ] Any contact lenses / glasses + spare
- [ ] Adhesive bandages and basic first aid
- [ ] Face masks (commonly worn in public)

### 4. Clothing by Season
**Spring (Mar–May): mild, layers, occasional rain**
- [ ] Light jacket or cardigan
- [ ] Long-sleeve tops + a few short-sleeve
- [ ] Compact umbrella
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes

**Summer (Jun–Aug): hot, humid, rainy season early on**
- [ ] Breathable short-sleeve shirts
- [ ] Shorts / light trousers
- [ ] Sun hat and sunglasses
- [ ] Compact umbrella (rain + sun)
- [ ] Quick-dry socks and a spare shirt for sweat

**Autumn (Sep–Nov): mild cooling, layers**
- [ ] Light to medium jacket
- [ ] Long-sleeve layers
- [ ] Scarf for cooler evenings
- [ ] Comfortable walking shoes

**Winter (Dec–Feb): cold, snowy in the north and mountains**
- [ ] Warm coat
- [ ] Thermal base layers
- [ ] Gloves, hat, scarf
- [ ] Warm socks
- [ ] Waterproof shoes / boots for snowy areas

### 5. Footwear & Bags
- [ ] One pair of broken-in walking shoes (you'll walk a lot)
- [ ] Slip-on shoes (you remove shoes indoors at many places)
- [ ] Spare socks (no holes — shoes come off indoors)
- [ ] Day bag / small backpack
- [ ] Foldable tote for shopping
- [ ] Packing cubes

### 6. Japan-Specific Items
- [ ] IC transit card (Suica / ICOCA / PASMO) or plan to buy on arrival
- [ ] Coin purse for vending machines and small shops
- [ ] Reusable shopping bag (plastic bags are often charged)
- [ ] Small foldable bag for trash (public bins are scarce)
- [ ] Compact umbrella
- [ ] Offline maps + translation app downloaded before the trip
- [ ] Slippers or socks for ryokan / temple visits
- [ ] Cash envelope for entrance fees and small vendors

### 7. Optional / Comfort
- [ ] Travel pillow and eye mask
- [ ] Refillable water bottle
- [ ] Snacks for travel days
- [ ] Laundry bag + travel detergent
- [ ] Mini sewing kit
- [ ] Notebook and pen

---

### Last-Minute Checks (day of departure)
- [ ] Phone fully charged + power bank charged
- [ ] Passport and tickets in carry-on
- [ ] Liquids under limit in clear bag
- [ ] Home: lights off, appliances unplugged, doors locked
- [ ] Check-in done / boarding pass saved
