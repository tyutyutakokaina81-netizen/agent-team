### Page 2 — How Winter in Japan Works (Read First)
Winter is roughly December through February, but conditions vary enormously by region. The right plan depends on which kind of winter you're chasing.

- **The north and mountains get serious snow.** Northern and high-elevation areas can have heavy, reliable snowfall — great for skiing and snow scenery, but expect cold and possible transport delays.
- **Much of the Pacific side is cold but drier.** Many major cities see crisp, often sunny winter days with little snow, which is easier for sightseeing.
- **Seasonal events have fixed windows.** Illuminations, snow festivals, and some onsen towns run only for set dates that change yearly — always confirm current dates.
- **Daylight is short.** Plan outdoor activities earlier; evenings are long, which is perfect for illuminations and onsen.
- **Closures happen.** Some seasonal spots, trails, and ferries pause in winter. Build buffer time.

> Hedge tip: leave at least one flexible buffer day per week for snow delays or closures.

### Page 7 — Onsen Etiquette Quick-Guide
General etiquette for hot springs. Customs vary by facility — read posted signs.

- **Wash first.** Rinse and wash thoroughly at the shower stations before entering the bath.
- **No swimwear** in most traditional baths; a small towel is for washing, not the water.
- **Tie up long hair** and keep towels out of the bath water.
- **Quiet and calm.** No splashing, swimming, or loud talk.
- **Tattoos:** some facilities restrict them — check the venue's current policy or look for tattoo-friendly options.
- **Hydrate** before and after; don't soak too long if you feel lightheaded.
