Page 3 — Trip Overview & How to Use
Kansai is Japan's cultural heartland: Kyoto for temples, gardens, and tradition; Osaka for nonstop food and energy. The two cities sit about 15 minutes apart by rapid train, so you can base yourself in one and day-trip easily.

How to use this planner:
- Follow the 5-day plan as-is, or mix and match the area pages.
- Use the budget tracker before and during your trip.
- Tick off the packing list the night before you fly.
- Keep the phrases page on your phone for quick reference.

Best base: Stay near a major station (Kyoto Station or Osaka/Namba/Umeda) for easy transfers.

Page 13 — Getting Around: Shinkansen / IC / Passes

| Option | What it is | Good for |
|---|---|---|
| Shinkansen | High-speed bullet train | Long hops (e.g., to/from Tokyo) |
| Rapid trains | Frequent regional trains | Kyoto ↔ Osaka (~15 min) |
| IC card | Rechargeable tap card | Trains, subways, buses, shops |
| City subway/bus | Local networks | Inside Kyoto / Osaka |
| Rail passes | Regional unlimited-ride passes | Multiple day trips |

Plan ahead:
- Get an IC card on arrival for tap-and-go travel.
- Compare a regional pass vs. single tickets based on your day trips.
- Reserve Shinkansen seats in busy seasons.
- Fares and pass coverage change — check the latest official info before buying.
