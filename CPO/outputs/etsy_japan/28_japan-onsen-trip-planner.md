Page 4 — Onsen Basics
- Onsen = a natural hot-spring bath. A public bathhouse heated with tap water is a "sento" — etiquette is similar.
- Baths are usually communal and gender-separated; you bathe without swimwear after washing first.
- A ryokan is a traditional inn, often with its own onsen, tatami rooms, and a multi-course dinner and breakfast.
- A "private / family bath" can sometimes be reserved if you prefer to bathe privately or as a group.

Page 5 — Bathing Etiquette — Step by Step
| Step | What to do |
|---|---|
| 1 | Remove shoes where indicated; enter the changing room. |
| 2 | Undress fully; put clothes in a basket or locker. |
| 3 | Take only your small towel into the bathing area. |
| 4 | Sit at a wash station; rinse and wash your whole body first. |
| 5 | Rinse off all soap completely before entering the bath. |
| 6 | Enter the bath slowly; keep your towel and hair out of the water. |
| 7 | Soak calmly; no swimming, splashing, or loud talking. |
| 8 | Step out, wipe down with your towel, then re-enter the changing room. |
| 9 | Dry off before dressing so the floor stays dry. |
- Hydrate before and after; don't soak too long if you feel dizzy.

Page 6 — Onsen Do & Don't
| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Wash thoroughly before getting in | Don't get in without washing first |
| Tie up long hair | Don't dip your hair or towel in the water |
| Keep your voice low | Don't swim, splash, or play |
| Fold your small towel on your head or the side | Don't bring the towel into the water |
| Drink water and rest between soaks | Don't soak so long you feel faint |
| Re-rinse if you sweated a lot | Don't take phones/cameras into the bath area |
- Note: Always follow each facility's posted house rules — they take priority.

Page 7 — The Towel Guide
Most onsen use two towels — know which is which.
| Towel | Size | Where it goes |
|---|---|---|
| Small "tenugui" towel | hand-towel size | into the bathing area; for washing & modesty; rest it on your head or the side — never in the water |
| Large bath towel | full size | stays in the changing room to dry off |
- Many ryokan provide both in your room; day-use baths may rent or sell a small towel.
- Pack a quick-dry small towel as a backup.
