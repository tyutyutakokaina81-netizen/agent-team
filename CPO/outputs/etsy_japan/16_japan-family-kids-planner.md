### Page 2 — Plan for a Kid-Friendly Pace (Read First)
Traveling with kids works best when the plan leaves room to breathe.

- **Fewer stops, more time.** Pick one or two main things per day, not five.
- **Build in breaks.** Snacks, rest, and downtime prevent meltdowns.
- **Anchor the day** around naps, meals, and energy levels.
- **Mix it up.** Pair a "parent" sight with something the kids will love.
- **Have a backup** indoor option for rain or tired days.
- **Stay near transit** so you're never far from getting back to base.

**Family pace check:**
- [ ] Max 1–2 main activities per day
- [ ] Rest/buffer time scheduled
- [ ] Meals and nap times accounted for
- [ ] Backup indoor option per day

### Page 4 — Getting Around with Kids
**Transport tips:**
- **IC transit cards** make trains, subways, and buses easy — kids' fare rules vary, so confirm.
- **Avoid rush hours** with strollers and small children where possible.
- **Elevators/escalators** exist at many stations but not all — check routes ahead for step-free access.
- **Priority seating** is available for those who need it.
- **Trains are quiet** — bring soft toys, books, or downloaded shows on headphones.

**Stroller vs. carrier:**
| | Stroller | Carrier |
|--|----------|---------|
| Best for | Long walks, naps on the go | Stairs, crowds, narrow spots |
| Watch out for | Stairs, crowded trains | Parent fatigue, heat |

- [ ] Plan step-free routes where you can
- [ ] Fold the stroller on busy trains/buses
- [ ] Keep a carrier handy as backup
- [ ] Note nearest restrooms with changing facilities

> Confirm step-free access and facilities for your specific stations and venues.

### Page 9 — Comfort & Safety Checklist
- **Agree on a meeting point** at each busy place in case anyone gets separated.
- **Carry ID info for kids** (accommodation name/contact) on them or in a pocket.
- **Teach kids to find staff** (uniformed station/shop staff) if lost.
- **Keep emergency contacts saved** — confirm current local emergency numbers before you travel.
- **Watch for crowds** near platform edges, escalators, and busy crossings.
- **Heat & hydration** — pace outdoor time and keep water handy in warm months.
- **Medication & allergies** — carry documentation; confirm any restrictions in advance.
- **Travel insurance** is strongly recommended for the whole family.
