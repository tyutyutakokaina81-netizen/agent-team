Page 5 — How Foliage Timing Works
- Autumn color generally appears earlier in the north and in higher mountains, and later in lower-elevation and more southern areas.
- Within a single region, mountains and valleys can peak at different times, so a "miss" at one spot may be a "hit" nearby at another elevation.
- Cooler nights tend to bring out color; warm autumns can delay it. Weather, wind, and rain near peak can shorten the best window.
- Action steps:
  - [ ] Check current-year foliage forecasts for your regions
  - [ ] Note typical (not guaranteed) peak ranges as a rough guide
  - [ ] Keep one or two elevation/region alternatives ready
- Reminder: timing is never guaranteed — plan to enjoy the trip even if peak shifts.

| Central mountains / Japanese Alps | High peaks and plateaus | Mid, varies by elevation |
| Kanto & around the capital | Gardens, parks, temple grounds | Mid–late |

| Western & southern Japan | Lower and milder areas | Latest |
