### Page 2 — How the Golden Route Works (Read First)
The Golden Route links Japan's most famous first-trip stops in a logical west-bound line, so you rarely backtrack.

- **The flow:** Start in Tokyo (big city + day trips), pause in Hakone (mountains, hot springs, views), move to Kyoto (temples, tradition), and finish in or near Osaka (food, nightlife, airport access).
- **One direction.** Travelling roughly Tokyo → Osaka means you keep moving forward and pack/unpack fewer times.
- **Base smart.** You don't need a hotel in every town — many travelers base in Kyoto and day-trip nearby.
- **Reserve key transport early.** Long-distance trains and popular stays fill up; book ahead and confirm current fares.
- **Pace it.** Seven days is enough to enjoy the highlights, not to see everything — choose depth over a checklist.

> Tip: keep one lighter "buffer/rest" half-day so the week doesn't burn you out.

- **Plan ahead:** Arrange forwarding the day before; delivery often takes a day or more — confirm timing locally.
