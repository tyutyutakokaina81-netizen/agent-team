### Page 2 — Where Your Money Goes (Read First)
Most travel spending in Japan falls into four buckets. Knowing the split helps you decide where to cut.

| Category | Why it adds up | Biggest lever |
|----------|----------------|---------------|
| Transport | Long-distance trips are pricey | Passes, slower routes, fewer hops |
| Accommodation | Nightly cost over many nights | Hostels, capsules, location, timing |
| Food | Three meals a day | Convenience stores, set meals, local spots |
| Activities | Entry fees and extras | Free sights, walking, free viewpoints |

**The budget mindset:**
- Set a realistic **daily target**, not just a total.
- Spend where it matters to *you*; cut everywhere else.
- Slower and simpler is usually cheaper.
- Track as you go so a few big days don't blow the trip.

> ⚠️ Every figure changes by season and provider. Confirm current prices before relying on any number.

### Page 3 — Transport: How to Save
Transport is often the single biggest cost, so it's the best place to plan carefully.

**Tactics:**
- [ ] Compare a **rail pass** vs. paying per trip for your actual route (see Page 4)
- [ ] Group long-distance trips to make a pass worthwhile, or skip it for single-city stays
- [ ] Use **IC transit cards** for local trains, subways, and buses
- [ ] Consider slower/cheaper train types or buses for some legs
- [ ] Look at overnight options that also save a night's lodging (confirm comfort/availability)
- [ ] Walk or cycle for short distances
- [ ] Base yourself centrally to cut daily transport

**Decision rule:** Add up your planned long-distance fares. If they're clearly above a pass price for the same coverage, a pass may pay off. If you're mostly in one city, per-ride with an IC card is often cheaper.

> Always check current pass terms, coverage, and eligibility — they change.

### Page 5 — Budget Accommodation Guide
Plenty of clean, safe, low-cost options exist if you're flexible.

| Option | Good for | Trade-off |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Hostels / guesthouses | Solo & social travelers | Shared spaces |
| Capsule hotels | Short city stays | Compact, minimal |
| Budget business hotels | Privacy on a budget | Small rooms |
| Dorm beds | Lowest cost | Less privacy |

**Save more:**
- [ ] Book earlier for better rates (especially busy seasons)
- [ ] Stay slightly outside the busiest central areas, near transit
- [ ] Choose places with free amenities (WiFi, simple breakfast, laundry)
- [ ] Travel in off-peak periods where possible
- [ ] Check cancellation terms before booking non-refundable deals

> ⚠️ Prices spike in peak seasons and around major events. Confirm current rates and policies.

### Page 6 — Food: Eat Well for Less
You can eat well in Japan without spending much.

**Cheap-eats checklist:**
- [ ] **Convenience stores** — surprisingly good, cheap meals and snacks
- [ ] **Set meals / lunch specials** — often cheaper than dinner for the same food
- [ ] **Standing or counter spots** for quick, affordable bowls
- [ ] **Supermarket deals**, including end-of-day discounts
- [ ] **Bakeries** for cheap breakfasts
- [ ] **Tap water** is generally fine — carry a reusable bottle
- [ ] **Food courts** in shopping areas for variety on a budget

**Money-savers:**
- Lunch out, lighter dinner from a store.
- Share dishes if dining as a group.
- Skip pricey drinks; water or convenience-store drinks cost less.

> Confirm any dietary or allergy needs directly — translation help is useful here.
