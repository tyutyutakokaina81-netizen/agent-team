### Page 2 — How Sakura Season Works (Read First)
Cherry blossom season is not a single fixed week. It moves across the country and shifts from year to year with the weather, so flexibility is your best tool.

- **It moves south to north.** Blossoms generally open earlier in warmer southern areas and progressively later as you go north and into higher elevations.
- **The peak window is short.** From first opening to full bloom is usually only a handful of days, and full bloom often lasts roughly a week before petals fall — weather (rain, wind, warmth) can shorten it.
- **Timing shifts yearly.** A warm or cold spell can move the peak earlier or later than a typical year. Treat any "usual" window as a rough guide only.
- **Different varieties bloom at different times.** Some trees open earlier and some later, which can stretch the overall viewing period in a given area.

**Strategy:** Pick a general window, build a flexible route, and have a backup region slightly earlier or later in the season so you can adjust toward wherever is closest to peak.

> ⚠️ Always confirm the current year's bloom updates from official local sources before locking in dates. Do not book non-refundable plans assuming a specific peak date.

### Page 3 — How to Read Bloom Timing
Common terms you'll see in updates:

| Term | What it usually means |
|------|----------------------|
| First bloom / opening | The first few blossoms have opened on a benchmark tree in that area |
| Full bloom | The large majority of blossoms are open — the prime viewing window |
| Petal fall / late bloom | Petals are dropping; still scenic, with "blossom snow" |

**Tips for using updates:**
- [ ] Check official local tourism or city sources for your target areas
- [ ] Look for *both* first-bloom and full-bloom estimates
- [ ] Re-check a week or two before you travel — estimates get refined as the date nears
- [ ] Build buffer days so you can shift toward an area near peak
- [ ] Remember higher-elevation and northern spots peak later — a useful "plan B"

_All timing is an estimate. Weather can change everything. Confirm the latest before relying on any date._

### Page 6 — Hanami (Blossom Viewing Picnic) Planner
Hanami is the tradition of relaxing under the blossoms. A little planning makes it smooth.

**Pick your spot:**
- [ ] Confirm whether the park/area allows picnics and any rules
- [ ] Check opening hours (some spots have evening illumination — confirm dates)
- [ ] Have a backup nearby in case it's crowded
- [ ] Note nearest station/transport and restrooms

**Picnic kit checklist:**
- [ ] Ground sheet / mat
- [ ] Snacks & drinks (buy locally for convenience)
- [ ] Wet wipes & hand sanitizer
- [ ] Trash bag (bins are scarce — carry your trash out)
- [ ] Layers / blanket (spring evenings get cool)
- [ ] Reusable bag, small cushion
- [ ] Camera / phone + charged battery

**Etiquette:**
- Don't shake, pull, or climb trees, or pick blossoms.
- Keep noise reasonable and clean up completely.
- Don't block paths; leave space for others.
- Follow any posted rules for the specific park.

### Page 9 — Crowd & Comfort Tips
- **Go early or late.** Popular spots fill up midday; early mornings and weekday visits are calmer.
- **Weekdays beat weekends** for crowds where your schedule allows.
- **Evening illuminations** can be magical but busy — confirm whether and when they run.
- **Have a plan B spot** nearby for every main spot.
- **Pace yourself.** Don't pack too many spots into one day; blossoms reward slow strolls.
- **Weather matters.** A rainy or windy day can speed petal fall — be ready to flex.
