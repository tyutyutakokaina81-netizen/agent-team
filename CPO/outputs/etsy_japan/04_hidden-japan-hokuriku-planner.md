Page 2 — Why Hokuriku?
Most first-time visitors do Tokyo and Kyoto and never look further. Hokuriku — the region facing the Sea of Japan — is the quieter Japan: a craft town casting bronze the same way for 400 years, a fishing port famous for winter yellowtail, mountain villages with thatched roofs, and one of the world's great alpine crossings.

Why it works:
- Kanazawa is a Shinkansen hub; Toyama and Takaoka are ~20–40 minutes away.
- Far fewer crowds than the golden route.
- Rare regional food you won't find elsewhere.
- Easy to combine with a standard Tokyo/Kyoto trip.

Page 12 — Himi & Toyama Bay Food

Toyama Bay is unusually deep, which is why its seafood is so prized.

Must-try:
- [ ] White shrimp (shiro-ebi) — sweet, delicate, a Toyama specialty
- [ ] Winter yellowtail (kan-buri) — rich cold-season fish
- [ ] Firefly squid (hotaru-ika) — spring delicacy
- [ ] Fresh sashimi rice bowls
- [ ] Local kelp-cured dishes

| When | What's in season |
|---|---|
| Spring | Firefly squid |
| Summer | White shrimp peak |
| Autumn | Mixed catch |
| Winter | Yellowtail |

Note: Seafood availability and prices are seasonal — check the latest before planning a food-focused trip.

Page 13 — Gokayama & Tateyama-Kurobe

Gokayama:
- Thatched-roof (gassho-zukuri) mountain villages
- Traditional washi paper-making
- Quiet, less-visited than the better-known village nearby

Tateyama-Kurobe Alpine Route:
- A multi-leg crossing of the Northern Japan Alps
- Cable cars, ropeway, and high-altitude buses
- Spring snow corridor; large dam; alpine panoramas
- Seasonal — confirm opening dates and reserve in advance

Page 14 — Getting Around: Shinkansen / IC / Passes / Alpine Route

| Option | What it is | Good for |
|---|---|---|
| Shinkansen | High-speed train | Reaching Kanazawa / Toyama |
| Local & regional trains | Frequent connections | Hopping between towns |
| IC card | Rechargeable tap card | Trains, trams, buses, shops |
| City trams | Local lines | Toyama / Takaoka sightseeing |
| Regional passes | Area unlimited-ride passes | Multi-stop itineraries |
| Alpine Route tickets | Multi-leg crossing pass | Tateyama-Kurobe day |

Plan ahead:
- Get an IC card on arrival.
- A regional pass can pay off across several days — compare vs. single fares.
- Book Alpine Route legs early in peak season; it is closed in winter.
- Schedules, fares, and pass coverage change — check the latest official info.

Page 15 — Avoid-the-Crowds Tips
- [ ] Start gardens and temples at opening time (08:00–09:00)
- [ ] Visit craft towns on weekdays
- [ ] Do the Alpine Route on the earliest legs of the day
- [ ] Base in Kanazawa and day-trip outward to dodge tour-bus timing
- [ ] Eat lunch slightly early (11:30) at popular seafood spots
- [ ] Save the famous village for the shoulder season; choose the quieter hamlets
- [ ] Travel mid-week between cities when possible
