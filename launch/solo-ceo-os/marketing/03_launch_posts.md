## Reddit — r/Entrepreneur / r/SideProject / r/Solopreneur

**Title:** I gave my AI six "executive roles" instead of one chat — here's what
changed running a one-person business

**Body:**
I kept using AI as a single assistant and getting shallow, generic output. So I
restructured it like a company: six roles (marketing, product, finance, sales,
tech, analytics), each with its own focused prompt, its own work log for memory,
and clear boundaries.

Two things made the difference:

1. **Roles** — a marketing question gets a marketing brain, not a generalist
   that drifts. The "CFO" role even pushed back on my pricing, which a normal
   chat never did.
2. **Memory** — each role logs what it did and reads it next time. Output
   compounds instead of resetting every session.

Happy to share the 1-page blueprint (org chart + the first role prompt) if
useful — link in a comment so I'm not spammy. Curious how others structure AI
for solo businesses.
