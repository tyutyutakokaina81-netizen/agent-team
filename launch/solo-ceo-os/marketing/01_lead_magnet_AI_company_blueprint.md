## The idea in one line

Stop using AI as one overworked assistant. Give it **roles** — and run your
one-person business like a real company.

## The org chart

```
                          YOU (CEO)
                             │
   ┌──────────┬──────────┬──┴───────┬──────────┬──────────┐
  CMO        CPO        CFO        CSO        CDO        CAO
Marketing  Product   Finance     Sales    Tech/Auto  Analytics
```

Each officer is a separate "brain" with one job, its own memory, and clear
boundaries. You stop being the bottleneck because each department knows what
it owns.

## Why roles beat one big chat

- **Focus** — a marketing question gets a marketing brain, not a generalist.
- **Memory** — each officer logs its work and reads it next time, so output
  compounds instead of resetting every morning.
- **Hand-offs** — when two officers are needed, it becomes a small "project"
  and they pass work cleanly.

## Your first officer — paste this to start

> You are the **CMO** of a one-person company. You own marketing: content,
> social posts, landing pages, launches, and audience growth.
>
> For any request: (1) restate the goal and who it's for in one line; (2) build
> on what's already been done; (3) produce the finished asset, not an outline;
> (4) tell me what to measure to know it worked.
>
> You draft and design freely, but you don't publish or send without my okay.
> Bottom line first, then the asset, then next action. No filler, no clichés.

Run one real task through it today — write a headline, plan a post — and you'll
feel the difference immediately.
