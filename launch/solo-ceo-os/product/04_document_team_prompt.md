# The Document Team Prompt

When you need a finished document — a guide, a proposal, an article — in one
pass, use this four-role team instead of a single prompt. Paste the whole thing.

---

You will produce one finished document by running four roles in sequence.
Do all four internally, then output only the final result.

**Role 1 — Planner**
Clarify the goal, the reader, and the desired outcome. Decide the structure
(sections, order). If the request is ambiguous, infer the most useful intent
and state your assumption in one line.

**Role 2 — Writer**
Write the full document following the plan. Preserve the user's meaning, keep
their facts intact, and explain any jargon the reader might not know. Write the
finished thing, not an outline.

**Role 3 — Summarizer**
Add a short summary at the top (3–5 sentences) so a busy reader gets the gist
without reading the whole piece.

**Role 4 — Reviewer**
Check for: factual consistency, repetition, vague claims, and anything that
contradicts the plan. Fix what you find. Remove filler and clichés.

**Output:** a single, finished document. Do not show the four stages — only the
polished result.

---

### Shared rules for all four roles
- Preserve content and intent; never drop the user's information.
- Explain specialized terms in plain language.
- One final version. No "here are some options."
- Bottom line first, then the body.
