## Description

If you run a business by yourself, your information tends to scatter: contacts in one
app, tasks in another, money in a spreadsheet, content ideas in your head. This kit
brings the essentials into a single, connected Notion workspace so you can see what
matters without switching tools.

It's a practical operating system, not a dashboard you'll abandon in a week. Seven
linked pages and databases are designed to work together — clients feed into projects,
projects feed into income tracking, and a short weekly review keeps everything current.

Because no two solo businesses are identical, this is delivered as a **complete,
documented specification** rather than a locked file you can't change. You get every
page, every field, the exact column types, recommended views, and plain-English
explanations of each formula — so you can rebuild it in your own workspace in minutes
and adjust anything to fit how you actually work. CSV files are included so the
database pages import in seconds.

### What's inside

- **Dashboard** — one screen with live views of this week's tasks, active clients, this month's money, and upcoming content.
- **Clients / CRM** — a simple pipeline with statuses, follow-up dates, and revenue rolled up per client.
- **Projects & Tasks** — projects and their tasks in one place, with by-status, by-deadline, and calendar views.
- **Income & Expenses** — a single ledger with monthly net rollups; no spreadsheet needed.
- **Content & Marketing calendar** — plan and track what you publish, by channel and date.
- **Goals & Weekly Review** — quarterly goals with progress, plus a repeatable Friday review template.
- **Templates Library** — ready-to-edit proposal, invoice cover note, and meeting-notes formats.

### How it's delivered

- A detailed Markdown spec (every page, property, type, view, and formula in plain English).
- A 15-minute step-by-step build guide.
- CSV files with example rows for each database page (import-ready).
- 2–3 example rows per database so you start with something real, not a blank page.

## Who it's for

- Freelancers and consultants juggling a handful of clients at once.
- Solo service providers (design, writing, dev, coaching) who want one home base.
- People starting a side business who want structure without buying five subscriptions.
- Notion users who'd rather understand and own their setup than unlock a black-box file.

## Who it's *not* for

- Teams needing multi-user permissions and approvals.
- Anyone wanting fully automated bookkeeping or accounting compliance — this is organization, not accounting software.
