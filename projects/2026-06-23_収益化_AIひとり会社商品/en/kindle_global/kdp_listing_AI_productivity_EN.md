## 2. Book Description (~150 words)

> Paste into the KDP "Description" field. Plain text below; light HTML (bold, line breaks) can be added in KDP's editor. No hype, no guarantees.

By the end of the day you have been busy without pause, yet the work that mattered is still waiting. This book is about using AI assistants — tools like ChatGPT and Claude — to take some of that friction away, so more of your energy reaches the work itself.

This is a practical, honest guide for busy professionals. It will not promise that AI does your job for you or that anyone can effortlessly produce brilliant work. Instead, it shows a disciplined approach: the AI drafts, you stay the editor. Across email, documents, slides, spreadsheets, research, meetings, and translation, every chapter gives you concrete steps, copy-paste prompt examples with clear placeholders, and a short checklist.

You will also learn what NOT to delegate — handling privacy, spotting confident-but-wrong answers, and keeping judgment and accountability where they belong: with you.

## 5. Recommended Price and Royalty Rationale

**Recommended list price (Amazon.com): $5.99 USD**

**Rationale:**
- The KDP 70% royalty tier requires a list price between **$2.99 and $9.99** on Amazon.com. Pricing at **$5.99** sits comfortably inside that band, so the book earns the 70% rate rather than the 35% rate that applies outside it.
- At $5.99, the 70% royalty (minus a small per-MB delivery cost — negligible for a text-only manuscript) yields roughly **$4.10–$4.15 per sale**, versus about **$2.10** if priced at $5.99 under the 35% tier or if priced below $2.99.
- $5.99 positions the title as a substantive, practical guide (16k–19k+ words) without straying into impulse-buy territory ($2.99–3.99, which can signal "thin") or premium territory ($7.99+, which raises buyer expectations and slows first-reviews velocity for a new author).
- **Launch tactic (optional):** introduce at **$3.99** for the first 1–2 weeks to gather early reads and reviews, then raise to **$5.99**. Both prices remain in the 70% band, so royalty rate is unaffected by the change.
- Set the same price in other major marketplaces using local equivalents (e.g., £4.99 / €5.99) rather than raw currency conversion, and enroll in KDP Select only if the exclusivity trade-off (Kindle Unlimited page reads) suits your goals; that decision is independent of the price logic above.

## 7. Cover Brief

> ⚠️ I cannot generate images. This is a brief you (or a designer / an image tool you operate yourself) can execute. It includes composition, text, color, and a ready-to-use AI image prompt.

**Format specs (KDP eBook cover):**
- Recommended size: **1600 × 2560 px** (1.6:1 height:width ratio), RGB, JPG or TIFF.
- Design for the thumbnail first: the title must be legible at ~150 px wide in search results.

**Concept and mood:**
- Calm, clean, professional, modern. The book's promise is *quiet* relief from friction, not flashy hustle — the cover should feel uncluttered and trustworthy, the visual opposite of busy.
- Avoid cliché AI imagery (glowing blue robot heads, circuit-board brains, humanoid androids). These signal "generic AI book" and undercut the honest, practical positioning.

**Composition (recommended):**
- Strong typographic cover with one simple, restful visual element. Two directions:
  - **Direction A (recommended):** Clean off-white or soft warm background; a single understated visual metaphor — e.g., a tidy desk corner viewed from above with a notebook, pen, and a coffee cup, or a calm, minimal abstract shape suggesting "space cleared." Large, confident title typography in the upper or central third.
  - **Direction B:** Pure typographic cover — bold title, no illustration — using generous whitespace and one accent color. Very readable as a thumbnail, low production cost.

**Text on cover:**
- **Title:** The Quiet Productivity Edge (largest element, top or center)
- **Subtitle (shortened for cover):** Use AI to Reclaim Hours in Everyday Knowledge Work
- **Author name:** {{Author name}} (bottom, smaller)
- Keep to **three text blocks maximum**. Do not put the full long subtitle on the cover.

**Color palette:**
- Background: soft warm neutral (off-white / pale sand `#F5F1E8`) or deep calm navy (`#1F2A37`).
- Accent: one restrained color — muted teal (`#2C7A7B`), warm amber (`#D69E2E`), or sage green (`#5F7A61`).
- Text: high contrast against background (deep charcoal `#1A202C` on light, or near-white `#F7FAFC` on dark). Ensure the title passes legibility at thumbnail size.

**Typography:**
- Title: a clean, confident sans-serif or a refined serif with good weight (e.g., a humanist sans or a modern serif). Avoid decorative/script fonts.
- Subtitle and author: lighter weight of the same family or a complementary sans-serif. Maintain clear hierarchy: title dominant, subtitle secondary, author tertiary.

**AI image prompt (if you generate the background/visual yourself):**
```

off-white surface, generous empty space on the left for text, soft natural
lighting, muted neutral palette with one subtle teal accent, no people, no
text, no logos, clean and uncluttered, professional editorial style,
shallow depth of field. High resolution, vertical orientation.
```
> If using an image tool, generate the visual only (no text), then add the title, subtitle, and author name in a layout/design tool so the typography is crisp and legible. Verify you have the rights to use any generated or stock image commercially on a book cover, per that tool's license.
