## 5. Price and Rationale

**Recommended list price: $4.99 USD** (within the $3.99–6.99 range).

Rationale:
- **$4.99 sits in the 70% royalty band** (KDP pays 70% on titles priced $2.99–$9.99 in eligible markets), so the author keeps a fair share while the price stays approachable for a beginner audience that is, by definition, watching its money.
- **It signals "real book, fair price"** — not a $0.99 throwaway (which can read as low-value in a scam-wary niche) and not a premium $6.99+ price that an unproven first-time author and a beginner audience may resist.
- **It is honest positioning:** the book's whole pitch is "do not overspend chasing income dreams." A modest, fair price reinforces that message; an aggressive price would contradict it.
- **Room to test:** launch at $4.99; consider a brief $2.99 or $0.99 promotional period for early reviews and ranking, then return to $4.99. Could test up to $5.99 later if reviews are strong, but do not start high.

> Set the same or equivalent price across major Amazon marketplaces; let KDP auto-convert where appropriate, then sanity-check that no marketplace lands at an odd or off-putting figure.

## 7. Cover Brief (instructions only — no image generated)

*Hand these notes to a cover designer or a cover tool. The cover must communicate one thing instantly: this is the honest, no-hype book in a category full of hype.*

**Core concept:** Calm, clean, trustworthy, modern. Deliberately the *opposite* of typical "make money with AI" covers (which use gold, cash, neon, rockets, and shouting). The contrast is the marketing. Restraint signals honesty.

**What to AVOID (critical — these would undercut the book's whole positioning):**
- No stacks of cash, gold coins, dollar signs, or money rain.
- No sports cars, mansions, beaches, or "laptop on a beach" imagery.
- No rockets, explosions, fire, or "to the moon" motifs.
- No glowing neon robot faces or sci-fi clichés.
- No fake urgency badges, no "#1 BESTSELLER" claims, no income figures.

**Recommended direction:**
- **Style:** Minimalist, editorial, confident. Plenty of clean negative space. Flat or near-flat design over photorealism.
- **Color palette:** Trustworthy and calm — deep navy or charcoal as a base, with a single warm accent (muted amber, soft teal, or a clean off-white). One or two colors, not many.
- **Typography:** Strong, clean sans-serif for the title; the word **"Honestly"** (or "Honest") emphasized — larger, or in the accent color — because that word is the entire hook. The subtitle smaller and clearly legible as a thumbnail.
- **Optional simple motif:** One restrained, conceptual element if any — e.g., a clean checkmark, a simple steady line/path, or a subtle nod to a tool/cursor — kept minimal and not cartoonish. A purely typographic cover (just bold text on a calm background) is a strong, on-brand option and often best for this niche.
- **Thumbnail test:** Must read clearly and look credible at small Amazon-thumbnail size. Title legible, "Honestly" prominent, no clutter. View it at thumbnail scale before finalizing.

**Mood in one line:** "The grown-up, honest book on the shelf, surrounded by shouting ones."

**Technical specs for KDP eBook cover:**
- Aspect ratio ~1.6:1 (height:width); recommended around 2,560 × 1,600 px.
- RGB color, JPG or TIFF, under 50 MB.
- Ensure all text is comfortably inside the safe area and crisp at small sizes.
- Author name and title must match the KDP metadata exactly.
