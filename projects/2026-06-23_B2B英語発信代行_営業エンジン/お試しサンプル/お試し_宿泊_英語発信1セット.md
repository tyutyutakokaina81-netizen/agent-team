## ② 英語の宿紹介記事 1本（予約判断に使える情報・約700語）

> 宿の公式サイト「About / Stay With Us」ページや、海外予約サイトの紹介欄に転載できる完成稿。住所・実在名は出さず、海外旅行者が「行けそう・泊まれそう」と判断できる文脈情報を入れています。

### A Quiet Riverside Stay in Western Toyama

Some places in Japan are built to impress you in the first five minutes. This is not one of them. Our inn is a small, family-run house beside a river in western Toyama, and it works slowly — the kind of place that feels ordinary when you arrive and hard to leave by the time you go.

**What kind of stay this is**

We have six rooms. That number matters: it means the common areas are rarely busy, the staff actually remember your name, and the rhythm of the house stays calm. If you're looking for a large hotel with a lobby bar, a gym, and round-the-clock service, this isn't the right match, and we'd rather tell you that honestly. If you want a quiet base for a night or two — somewhere to sleep well, eat well, and let western Japan unfold at walking pace — you'll feel at home here.

The rooms are simple in the Japanese sense: clean lines, natural materials, futon bedding laid out in the evening, and a window that looks toward the water. We provide towels, basic toiletries, tea, and Wi-Fi throughout. English-speaking guests are very welcome; while not all of our team speaks fluent English, we communicate clearly by phone and message before and during your stay, and we're used to helping international visitors.

**Getting here (the practical part)**

Western Toyama is more reachable than many travellers expect. The region connects to the national rail network, and from the nearest station our inn is a short drive away. Because the final stretch isn't always obvious to first-time visitors, we offer a simple pickup arrangement — just send us your arrival time in advance and we'll coordinate the details. Many guests come as part of a wider trip through central Japan; we're an easy overnight stop rather than a place you need to plan an entire holiday around.

If you're driving, parking is available on site. If you're arriving by train, let us know and we'll send clear, step-by-step directions in English ahead of time so there are no surprises at the end of a long travel day.

**Eating here**

Dinner is the heart of a stay with us. We serve a seasonal multi-course meal built around local ingredients — river fish, mountain vegetables, regional rice — prepared simply and served in courses. The menu shifts through the year because the produce does, so two visits in different seasons genuinely taste different. We're happy to accommodate dietary requirements, including vegetarian and common allergies, as long as you tell us when you book. Breakfast is a quieter affair: a warm, homemade Japanese set to start the day gently.

**What's around**

This is a neighbourhood for slowing down rather than ticking off sights. Within walking distance you'll find a riverside path that's especially pleasant before dinner, a handful of small local eateries, and the everyday texture of a Japanese town that most tourists never see. For guests who want more, the wider region rewards day trips: coastline, mountains, and small craft and food traditions are all within reach by car or local transport, and we're glad to help you plan a route at the front desk.

**Across the seasons**

Spring brings soft greenery and mild walking weather. Summer is alive and green, with long evenings by the water. Autumn turns the surrounding hills warm with colour and is, for many guests, the most beautiful time to visit. Winter is quiet and still — a good season for travellers who like a town to themselves and a warm room to return to.

**Before you book**

We're a small house, so rooms go quickly in peak seasons; booking ahead is wise, especially around autumn. Tell us your arrival time, any dietary needs, and whether you'd like a station pickup, and we'll handle the rest in clear English. We can't promise the busy, polished experience of a big hotel — but we can promise a calm room, an honest meal, and a quieter side of Japan that's well worth the detour.

We'd be glad to host you.

## ④ 狙いと使い方（日本語解説）

### このサンプルの狙い

宿泊業の海外集客でいちばん多い失敗は、**「予約サイトには載っているが、英語が機械翻訳のまま」**という状態です。海外ゲストは予約前に必ず紹介文とレビュー欄を読みますが、不自然な英語は「ここはちゃんと対応してもらえるのか？」という不安に直結します。逆に、自然で誠実な英語が並んでいるだけで、予約のハードルは確実に下がります。

このサンプルは、その「自然で誠実な英語」を、SNS・紹介記事・ゲスト返信という**予約導線の3点セット**で実演したものです。

### 飲食店版との違い（切り口の設計意図）

- 飲食店は「今日その場で行く理由」を作る発信。宿は**「数日〜数週間前に予約を決めてもらう」**発信。だから本サンプルは、衝動より**安心材料（アクセス・食事制限対応・季節）**を前面に出しています。
- SNS3本も「客室＝静けさ／食事＝土地の今／周辺＝過ごし方」と切り口を分け、同じ言い回しの反復を避けています。

### 使い方（営業フロー）

1. **営業文に添付**して送る（「貴施設の情報で同じものを1セット無料で作ります」と添える）。
2. 反応があれば、**この架空サンプルを貴施設バージョンに差し替えた無料デモ**を1セット提供。
3. 継続契約（月次の英語SNS運用＋紹介文整備）へ接続。

### 注意（厳守事項）

- 本サンプルは**架空の宿**を題材にした実演です。実在施設の情報・写真は使用していません。
- **来客増・予約増は保証しません。** お約束するのは「海外ゲストに自然に伝わる英語発信の継続的な品質」です。営業時もこの線を越える表現（「必ず満室」「インバウンド○倍」等）は使いません。
- 実制作時は、写真・客室数・送迎可否・食事内容など**事実は必ず施設に確認**してから英文化します（事実誤認は信用を損なうため）。
