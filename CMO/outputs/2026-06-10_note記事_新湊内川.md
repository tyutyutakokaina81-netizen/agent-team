🌏 For English readers

Shinminato's Uchikawa Canal is marketed as the "Venice of Japan" — which is technically a description of its layout, but sets up the wrong expectations.

It's not an architectural showpiece. It's a working fishing town built along a narrow canal that empties into Toyama Bay. Wooden houses sit close to the water. Fishing boats moor overnight a few meters from someone's front door. Early morning, the engines start and the boats head out.

The pleasure of walking here is the compression of the ordinary: fishing gear, low bridges, windows facing the canal, the smell of salt and diesel in the morning. A few hours on foot from bridge to bridge gives you a clear picture of how a Japanese fishing port town organizes its daily life.

From Takaoka, the Manyo Line tram runs toward the coast — about 40 minutes to an hour. Worth the ride.
