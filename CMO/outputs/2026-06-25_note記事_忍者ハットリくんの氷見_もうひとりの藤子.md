Doraemon's hometown is Takaoka — but 15 minutes further, Himi belongs to the OTHER Fujiko.

Everyone treks to Takaoka for the cat robot. Almost no one keeps going up the
coast to Himi, the fishing town where Fujiko Fujio Ⓐ — the darker, funnier half
of the original duo — was born. That's the hidden move: from Kanazawa, the
Hokuriku Shinkansen reaches Shin-Takaoka in about 15 minutes, then the little

Here's the part you don't need a ticket for. You don't enter Himi's Fujiko Ⓐ
world — you walk into it. Bronze ninjas crouch on shopping-street corners.
Manhole covers grin up at you. A clock tower hides characters that pop out on
the hour to fight a small, choreographed battle while shoppers carry home fish.
The whole town quietly cosplays a 1960s manga, and nobody's selling you
anything for it.

It smells of salt and grilled squid. It sounds like seagulls and a level
crossing. You came for one Fujiko and accidentally collected the other.

In Himi, the manga isn't behind glass — it's underfoot, around the corner,
waiting on the next hour.
