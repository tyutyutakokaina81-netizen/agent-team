Asked what souvenir to bring from Toyama, my default answer is
shiraebi senbei — white shrimp rice crackers.

The reason is simple: I've never met someone who didn't like them.

Shiraebi (white shrimp) are tiny translucent shrimp caught almost
exclusively in Toyama Bay. About 7cm long, they turn pink when
boiled. Fresh, they spoil quickly, so processed forms — sashimi
locally, or these crackers for travel — are how their flavor
spreads.

The crackers blend dried shrimp paste into rice cracker batter,
then bake until the umami turns to fragrance. Light, shelf-stable,
hard to break in luggage.

They have an addictive salt-oil-shrimp balance. One leads to
another. A few minutes of family sharing, and someone always
reaches for the second handful.

There are two kinds of regional souvenirs in Japan: the visually
striking ones that win social media, and the quietly reliable
ones that get bought again every visit. Shiraebi senbei sit
firmly in the second category. "Bring back the usual" is the
strongest review a souvenir can get.
