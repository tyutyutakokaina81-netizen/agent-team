## English (primary — travel writing)

```
The pilgrimage you didn't know you needed: Doraemon's hometown

I watched a grown man wait his turn — phone ready, a little shy — to
photograph a small statue of a blue robot cat. He wasn't alone. There was a
quiet line of adults from Indonesia, Vietnam, Thailand, all here in Takaoka
for the same reason: this is where Doraemon comes from.

Not the merchandise. The feeling. Fujiko F. Fujio, the artist who drew him,
grew up in this small city on the Sea of Japan, and you can feel his
fingerprints all over it. There's no theme-park gate, no entrance fee at
Otogi-no-Mori park — just statues among the trees and the strange weight of
standing somewhere a piece of your childhood was actually born.

Here's the thing nobody tells you: you came to Japan for temples and sushi,
but the part that makes your throat tight is a cartoon you watched at seven.
That's not embarrassing. That's the whole point of travel — going far to
meet your younger self.

Takaoka is 15 minutes from Kanazawa by Shinkansen, almost no tourists, no
queue but the gentle one of grown-ups remembering. Some places you visit.
Some you return to without ever having been.
```
