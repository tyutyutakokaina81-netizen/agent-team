A lot of people think of oysters as a winter food. Rock oysters — iwagaki —

They're really a separate thing from the small farmed oysters of winter that

strong taste of the sea floods your mouth.

On a hot day, well-chilled rock oysters over ice are hard to beat — cold,
salty, and rich, sinking into a body that's been sweating. Being wild, they
vary in size and fullness from day to day, and honestly I like that about
them. If you come to Toyama in summer, go looking for iwagaki. If you only
know winter oysters, the depth of this summer flavor may surprise you.
