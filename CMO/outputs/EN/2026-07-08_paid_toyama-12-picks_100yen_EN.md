I live in Takaoka, a small city on the Sea of Japan coast in central Japan,
about 20 minutes by local train from Toyama. People who visit keep asking me
the same thing: "I'm going to Toyama — but what do I actually eat, and where
do I actually go?" Guidebooks give you so much that you end up choosing nothing.

So I did the simple thing. I wrote down only what I'd take a friend to if they
came to stay — the food I'd order and the places I'd walk them through, nothing
I'd feel I had to explain away. It came to twelve. This isn't a "cover everything"
list; it's a "you won't regret these" list. Each one comes with a word you can
type straight into a map app (Google Maps and the like) to find the spot.

For scale: Toyama is one of the smaller prefectures on the Sea of Japan side, so
the food and the walks sit close together. You can be eating at a market in the
morning and standing in a quiet temple courtyard by early afternoon without a
long drive between them. That's the part I like most about showing people around
here — it doesn't ask much of your legs or your schedule.

A note on how I've written the locations: I don't give street addresses or the
names of specific shops. I use station, market and public-facility names, plus
general area names, so you can arrive without getting lost — and so I'm not
sending everyone to one small place.

■ How to use this list

- You don't have to go in order. Pick the ones that catch your eye, in whatever
  range you can cover.
- I've mixed "food" and "places to walk". It works as a half-day, or stretched
  across two or three nights.
- The search words go straight into a map app. Most of these are reachable by
  train, tram and walking — you don't need a car for the ones below.

■ First, three for free

1. [Food] Raw white shrimp (shiro-ebi) — a small shrimp caught in Toyama Bay.
   The sweetness is different from anything you'll have had, and locally it's
   often called the bay's "jewel" for its pale, almost see-through look. Have it
   raw as sashimi, or over rice as a small donburi if you'd rather a full meal.
   Map search word: "Toyama Station Kitokito Market"
2. [Walk] Amaharashi Coast — on a clear morning you can see the Tateyama
   mountain range across the water. Go early; the light is the whole point.
   Map search word: "Amaharashi Coast" / time of day: morning
3. [Walk] Takaoka's old copperware foundry streets — Takaoka has made cast
   metal for centuries, and one preserved district still has the old workshop
   frontages. Walk it in the late-afternoon light.
   Map search word: "Takaoka copperware old town" / time of day: late afternoon

Honestly, even just these three and Toyama becomes a "glad I came" trip.
But the ones a local really thinks you'd be missing out on are the next nine.

■ Here are the 12 a local won't skip

4. [Food] Masu-zushi (pressed trout sushi) — the classic Toyama train-station
   lunch box. Sold as a round wooden case; the acidity and thickness differ by
   maker, so it's worth trying more than one over a trip.
   Map search word: "Toyama Station masuzushi"
5. [Food] Beni-zuwai crab (red snow crab) — the everyday winter treat here.
   More affordable than the pricier snow crab, and the markets serve it simply.
   Map search word: "Shinminato Kittokito Market" / season: autumn to winter
6. [Food] Toyama Black ramen — a dark, strong soy-sauce ramen. It's salty on
   purpose; locals eat it with rice, not on its own. Try it once and decide.
   Map search word: "Toyama Black Toyama Station"
7. [Food] Kobujime — sashimi cured briefly between sheets of kelp. It's a
   Toyama food custom, the fish turns firmer and rounder in flavor, and the
   vacuum-packed kind travels home well as a gift.
   Map search word: "Toyama kobujime market"
8. [Food] Hotaru-ika (firefly squid) — a spring taste; the bay is known for
   these small squid that glow. The soy-marinated version is a good drinking
   snack. This one is genuinely seasonal, so check the timing.
   Map search word: "Namerikawa hotaruika" / season: spring
9. [Walk] Zuiryuji Temple, Takaoka — a National Treasure temple. What I like is
   how quiet it stays; you can usually walk it without crowds. Give it time.
   Map search word: "Zuiryuji Temple Takaoka"
10. [Walk] Toyama Glass Art Museum — it shares a building with the city library,
    and the architecture alone is worth the stop even before the glass works.
    Map search word: "Toyama Glass Art Museum"
11. [Walk] Fugan Canal Kansui Park — a walking path along a canal. Calm by day,
    and the evening lighting stays gentle rather than flashy. Easy to reach.
    Map search word: "Kansui Park Toyama"
12. [Walk] Otogi-no-Mori Park, Takaoka — a wide open park with a well-known
    cartoon-character statue in it. Good if you're traveling with kids, or just
    want green space and room to breathe.
    Map search word: "Otogi no Mori Park Takaoka"

+ A few local notes

- You can do most of this by train, tram and on foot. Takaoka to Toyama is
  about 20 minutes on the local line.
- For seafood, anchoring your day around a "market" is the safest bet
  (the markets near Shinminato and near Toyama Station).
- On a rainy day, rebuild the plan around the Glass Art Museum, the Kansui Park
  area and Zuiryuji Temple, and you'll stay mostly dry.
