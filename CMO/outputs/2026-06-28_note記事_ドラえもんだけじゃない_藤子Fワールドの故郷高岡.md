## English (primary — travel writing)

```
You came for Doraemon. The town actually made a whole universe.

The same man, from the same town — Hiroshi Fujimoto, pen name Fujiko F. Fujio,
born in Takaoka — did not just draw one cat. He built a universe, and most
travelers never realize how much of their childhood traces back to a single
snowy city on the Sea of Japan coast.

Kiteretsu, a boy genius reinventing his great-grandfather's inventions.
Esper Mami, a teenage girl waking up to quiet psychic powers. 21-Emon, a
hotel-boy in a far-flung space future. Obake no Q-taro, the clumsy ghost who
basically invented the "creature lives with a family" formula before Doraemon
perfected it. One imagination, one hometown, an entire shelf.

That's the trip's real payoff. Standing in Takaoka, you stop seeing a single
character and start seeing the workshop of a mind — the small-city longing for
elsewhere, for the future, for stranger powers, that runs through everything
he made. It reframes Doraemon, too: not a one-off, but the most famous door
in a much larger house.

Takaoka is 15 minutes from Kanazawa by Shinkansen, no reservation needed. Come
for one beloved cat. Leave realizing one person, in one town, quietly authored
a corner of half the world's childhood.
```
